## 1. Executive Summary

Ryan Realty does not appear in the top 10 for any of the 10 canonical commercial-intent queries. The site has 1,852 impressions and 18 clicks per month on the homepage alone, which means Google is indexing the pages and testing them in search — but the titles, metas, and schema are not converting impressions into clicks. The SERP landscape in Bend is dominated by two types of players: large-inventory aggregators (Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin, Trulia) on buyer-intent queries, and three local brokerages (Bend Premier, Cascade Hasson, and bendrealestate.com/High Desert Realty) on commercial-intent and sell-intent queries. Ryan Realty has genuine advantages — a licensed principal broker, verified MLS-sourced market data, and the strongest neighborhood-page content quality in the market — but none of those advantages are visible in SERPs because schema markup is absent, title tags don't carry differentiating data, and there is no dedicated seller-focused landing page.

The one competitor that demands a specific and direct response is Bend Premier Real Estate. They appear in 5 of the 10 canonical queries, have a content archive of 600-plus posts dating back to 2015, and have just formalized a "Women Who Lead" leadership structure that gives them a distinctive institutional angle. They are not outcompeting Ryan Realty on data accuracy or content depth per post — their posts are readable but thin, and their market activity numbers on the homepage appear to be unverified platform pulls (211 sold, 418 new listings, 365 homes on market, 104 avg DOM for April 2026 — figures that conflict with Ryan Realty's Supabase-verified dataset and should be treated as unchecked). Ryan Realty can beat them on the one thing they do not have: a clear, specific, data-traceable voice that shows its work.

The single biggest gap between Ryan Realty and the top SERP competitor on commercial-intent queries is a dedicated seller page. "Sell my home Bend" is dominated by cash-buyer sites and two or three brokerages. Ryan Realty has no page targeting that query. Creating `/sell-your-bend-oregon-home/` with a CMA offer above the fold and the May 2026 months-of-supply figure in the opening paragraph would put Ryan Realty on the board for the highest-conversion query in local real estate SEO.

## 4. Cross-Competitor Patterns

**What all of them do that Ryan Realty does not:**

1. **A dedicated seller landing page with a named CTA.** Every competitor has some version of `/sell/` or `/my-home-value/` or `/sell-your-home/`. Ryan Realty has `/sellers/` (per the SEO audit) and a free-home-valuation page but no page targeting the exact query "sell my home bend."
2. **An email subscription mechanism on content pages.** Bend Premier has a sidebar newsletter signup on every blog post. Duke Warner has category-level email capture. Ryan Realty has no visible email capture mechanism on any crawled page.
3. **Individual broker profile pages indexed separately.** Multiple competitors (bendrealestate.com, Duke Warner, High Desert) have individual broker pages with photos, bios, and review counts indexed separately from the main brokerage page. Ryan Realty's three brokers appear on `/about-us/` but do not have individual indexed pages. Individual broker pages rank for "[broker name] bend" queries — the Rebecca Palombo/Peterson name mismatch in GSC is a symptom of this gap.
4. **A monthly market trend report on a predictable cadence.** Duke Warner (monthly since 2015), Bend Premier (weekly/monthly), High Desert (download guide) all have structured, recurring market content. Ryan Realty has strong market content but it is not on a predictable publishing cadence visible from the site structure.

**What none of them do (white space):**

1. **Show their work on market data.** No competitor publishes a verification trace or cites a specific database, filter, and row count for the numbers they publish. Bend Premier's homepage shows "211 sold / 418 new / 365 on market / 104 avg DOM for April 2026" with no source. Duke Warner's trend report is described as "carefully compiled" with no methodology. High Desert's seller page references "real market data" without naming the source. Ryan Realty's CLAUDE.md-enforced citation standard (source + table + filter + row count + query text) is a content practice no competitor follows.
2. **Publish verified, methodology-shown neighborhood-level market data.** All competitors publish city-level data (Bend, Redmond). None crawled have neighborhood-specific data pages with verified inventory, median price, and days-on-market at the subdivision level with sources cited.
3. **Address the out-of-state seller directly as a named persona.** High Desert comes closest (seller FAQ). None name this persona explicitly or write content directly for someone who cannot physically be at the property. The voice guidelines' "Reference seller persona" section describes exactly what the market is missing: an agent who will physically show up, find contractors, and communicate weekly without being chased.
4. **A podcast or recurring audio content format.** NestBend has one; no one else in the top tier does. Video content (YouTube) is present for some competitors but not confirmed in the crawl set.

**What is emerging:**

The Haley Overton / Bend Home Collective Yahoo Finance placement (May 12, 2026) is the signal to watch. Press-release SEO for individual agents is beginning. If this tactic produces sustained page-1 rankings for "[agent name] + bend" queries, it will surface competing agent names more prominently on searches where Ryan Realty's brokers should be appearing. The Rebecca Peterson individual agent page gap (no indexed broker profile pages) is directly relevant.
