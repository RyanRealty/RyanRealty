## Winning Patterns

### Hook Formula (TikTok-Specific)

TikTok's qualified-view threshold sits at 3 seconds in 2025–2026 — a view only "counts" algorithmically if the viewer stays past 3 seconds. The hook must earn that in the first 2 seconds.

The formula that works across the top real estate creators:

1. **Motion by frame 0** — never start on black or a static title card
2. **One of four triggers by second 2:**
   - Specific number with stakes ("$3,200/month in Portland looks like this")
   - Pattern interrupt or contradiction ("The neighborhood nobody talks about is beating Westside on price")
   - Stakes-first storytelling ("This buyer lost the house. Here's what I'd do differently.")
   - Visual payoff — the most dramatic shot of the property, immediately
3. **Implicit question left open** — the viewer must need to know what happens next
4. **No intro, no name, no greeting** — "Hey guys welcome back" is a skip trigger

### Length Sweet Spot (2026)

Data across multiple sources converges on a range, not a single number:

- **Property tours:** 20–35 seconds. Fast cuts. Price transparent. Saves drive algorithm.
- **Educational / market commentary:** 60–90 seconds. Audience is patient if value is clear from second 2.
- **Storytelling / client anecdotes:** 60–120 seconds. Completion rate stays high if narrative tension is maintained.
- **Short-form hooks / meme / trend participation:** 15–25 seconds. High completion, high share.

**The 2026 shift:** The platform is rewarding longer content (60–180 seconds) over 2024's dominant 15-second clips, as TikTok competes with YouTube for watch time and ad inventory. But "longer" only works if pacing is relentless — no dead air, no filler, no slow reveals. Completion rate must hit 70%+ (up from 50% in 2024) for algorithmic distribution to expand.

**Bottom line for Ryan Realty:** 30–45 seconds for property/area reveals. 60–90 seconds for market data and educational content. Never shorter than 20 seconds (too shallow for the Bend market context that earns trust). Never longer than 90 seconds for this audience without a serialized narrative hook.

### Trending Audio Strategy

In 2026, TikTok treats audio as a content category — a video using a trending sound gets surface in the feed of users already engaging with that sound, even if they've never seen the account.

**The rules:**
- Use trending audio within the first **24–48 hours** of the sound's emergence for the biggest algorithmic lift
- Original audio is gaining ground — creator-backed soundtracks earn a separate audience pool and can become a brand asset if your sound gets used by others
- For VO-driven content (market reports, educational), trending audio in the background at low volume preserves the algorithmic signal without competing with the information
- For property tours and area videos, trending music at moderate volume works because the visual carries the content

**The 2026 nuance:** TikTok has stated a preference for original audio, and creators who build recognizable sonic branding (a signature sound, a familiar format) see stronger community formation over time. Madison Sutton and Glennda Baker both lean heavily on their own voice as the audio anchor.

### Captions (On-Screen Text)

TikTok has moved aggressively toward auto-captioning — the platform's built-in caption tool is free, accurate, and preferred by the algorithm because it makes content accessible and increases text-based search indexing.

- **Burned-in vs. auto-generated:** Both work. Auto-captions generated by TikTok's native tool are indexed for search (the speech-to-text engine also indexes your spoken words). Burned-in custom captions give more visual control but provide the same indexing if the audio is present.
- **On-screen text overlays** (separate from captions) should carry the primary message for muted viewers — 64% of short-form viewers watch without sound
- **Caption SEO:** TikTok's OCR scans all on-screen text and indexes it for search. If "Bend Oregon homes under $600,000" appears on screen, those words are searchable. Speak your keywords AND show them on screen.

### Aspect Ratio and Format

- **9:16 portrait, always.** No exceptions for TikTok-native content.
- Film vertically. Content filmed horizontally and cropped looks punished — narrow safe zone, clipped focal points, viewer immediately recognizes it as repurposed
- Safe zone for text: leave 10–15% margin on all edges. TikTok UI overlays (handle, caption bar, like/comment stack) consume the right edge and bottom

### Pacing

- **2–3 second cuts for property tours.** Longer for establishing exterior shots only.
- **No dead air, no slow pans** that don't reveal something new
- The rewatch rate is now TikTok's highest-weighted signal (above completion rate). Videos people rewatch once get a 2–3x distribution multiplier. Design for the rewatch: pack enough information that a second viewing is rewarding.
- Pattern interrupt at 25% and 50% of the video maintains completion rate

### Face vs. Voiceover

The data is clear: face-on-camera significantly outperforms voiceover-only for **agent brand building**. Face content converts followers to clients at higher rates because real estate is a relationship decision.

- **Show your face when:** building initial trust, explaining complex market information, telling client stories, commenting on your local market
- **Voiceover-only works for:** property tours (the home is the subject), area drone footage, market data explainers where visuals carry the story
- **The hybrid:** Most high-performing real estate creators open face-to-camera (establishing the person and the promise) then cut to b-roll or property footage for the middle, then return to face for the close or CTA

For Ryan Realty specifically: if Matt is the face of the content, on-camera presence builds the relationship that converts. A single recognizable face associated with the Bend market compounds over time — viewers who see you 7+ times are primed to contact you before any competitor they haven't seen.

### Branding Restraint — TikTok Specifically

TikTok's developer Content Posting API guidelines explicitly prohibit superimposing brand logos, watermarks, or promotional branding on video content. More practically, the culture of the platform actively punishes it:

- Heavy branded content feels like an ad, and TikTok users have exceptional ad-blindness
- The algorithm treats watermark-heavy, mass-produced-feeling content as low-quality and limits distribution
- "Raw, native-feeling content filmed vertically on smartphones" is the language used by TikTok's own business guides to describe what succeeds
- The analysis of top performing real estate TikTok creators confirms: **zero of the top 10 real estate accounts put a brokerage logo on their video frames**

**What minimal branding looks like on TikTok:**
- Handle visible on account profile (only)
- Name in bio
- Consistent visual style (color palette, font if using text overlays, tone of voice) that becomes recognizable without a logo
- No "Presented by," no brokerage name in the video frame, no phone number in video

**Quantified:** Based on analysis of the top 10 real estate TikTok creators, logo/brokerage branding appears in video frames approximately 0% of the time. Branding lives in the bio, not the video.

### Hashtag Strategy

TikTok's algorithm has de-emphasized hashtags relative to 2022–2023. In 2026, keywords in captions and spoken audio are indexed directly — hashtags are a supplemental signal, not the primary one.

**Practical rules:**
- 3–5 hashtags maximum. More than 5 is noise.
- Mix: 1 broad (#realestate or #realtor), 1–2 niche (#oregonrealestate, #bendoregon), 1 trending if relevant, 1 geographic specificity (#centraloregonahomes)
- Never use hashtags that don't match the video's content — TikTok's relevance matching punishes mismatched tags
- Put primary keywords in the first line of the caption (not as a hashtag — as a plain language phrase): "Bend Oregon homes under $700K — here's what the market looks like right now"

### Posting Cadence

- **Minimum:** 3 videos per week to register with the algorithm
- **Optimal:** 3–5 per week, consistently. Consistency outperforms volume.
- **Batch production:** Film 8–12 videos in a single 2-hour session weekly, then schedule across 7–10 days. This is how agents with active client workloads maintain TikTok presence without daily filming.
- Posting at the same time each day drives 40% higher growth rates (algorithm pattern recognition)
- Best single time to post: Sunday 9 AM. Best overall window: 6 AM–9 AM local time
- Respond to all comments within the first hour after posting — engagement velocity in that window determines initial distribution

### 2026 Algorithm Signals

Signal hierarchy from highest to lowest weight, verified across multiple 2026 algorithm analyses:

1. **Rewatch/loop rate** — the #1 signal. A viewer who watches twice sends a stronger signal than two viewers who watch once
2. **Completion rate** — must reach 70%+ for algorithmic expansion (up from ~50% in 2024)
3. **Shares** — highest-effort engagement action; signals the viewer is staking their social currency on your content
4. **Saves** — predict "future intent"; viewers save to return to useful content. TikTok treats saves as an indicator of authority and downstream action (profile visit, follow, DM)
5. **Meaningful comments** — comments with words (not emojis only) signal genuine reaction
6. **Follower conversion** — follows gained per video session

**Likes are the lowest-weighted signal.** A video with 500 saves and 1,000 likes outperforms a video with 50 saves and 10,000 likes.

**New in 2026:** TikTok tests videos with the creator's existing followers first, then expands based on how followers respond. This means your existing audience quality matters more than its size — a small, highly engaged audience gets content pushed further than a large, passive one.

**TikTok as search engine:** 64% of Gen Z and 49% of all US consumers now use TikTok as a primary search tool. TikTok indexes: spoken words (speech-to-text), on-screen text (OCR), caption keywords, and hashtags. The search surface is now a meaningful discovery channel independent of the FYP.
