### Winning Patterns

#### Reels Strategy (different from IG Reels)

Facebook Reels uses the same UTIS (User True Interest Survey) model as Instagram Reels since January 2026, but the discovery pool is different. Facebook skews 35–65+, which means:

- **15–30 seconds is the sweet spot** — Facebook users accept longer video than IG but completion rate drops sharply at 45s
- **Captions mandatory** — same muted-feed reality as IG; Facebook's auto-captions are acceptable quality but custom styling lifts engagement
- **October 2025 algorithm update:** Reels uploaded that same day receive 50% more distribution than archived or reposted content — freshness matters more on FB than IG
- **FB Reels tolerate more overt branding** than IG Reels because the audience expects it; a lower-third with agent name + brokerage does not penalize reach the way it can on TikTok or IG
- Content that works: market stat reveals ("Bend inventory just hit X — here's what that means"), local lifestyle ("why people are leaving Portland for Bend"), frank seller/buyer advice delivered direct-to-camera

#### Feed Post Pattern

- **4:5 vertical image or video** outperforms 1:1 for feed; takes up more screen real estate on mobile
- **Links in comments, not in posts** — FB suppresses posts with outbound links in the copy; put the link in the first comment after posting
- **Color-background text posts** perform well as pattern interrupts in image-heavy feeds (Mari Smith, Social Media Examiner verified)
- **Carousel/multi-image posts** generate high swipe engagement and dwell time; use for "before/after," neighborhood spotlights, market stat decks
- Caption length: 50–150 characters for peak performance on feed; say the insight, stop

#### Group Strategy

The highest-ROI organic Facebook play for a local broker:

1. **Create a community-first group** — not "Ryan Realty Listings," but "Bend Oregon Homeowners" or "Moving to Central Oregon" or "Bend Real Estate Q+A"
2. **Start public** to reach 500 members; then switch to private for quality and retention
3. **Content ratio: 90% value, 10% real estate** — answer questions, share local news, post restaurant recommendations; real estate drops naturally
4. **Cost: $0–$300/month** for organic + light boosting; expect 60–120 day ramp before leads appear
5. **Laura Griffin model:** 7-figure GCI attributed primarily to Facebook Groups by providing consistent local expertise without direct selling
6. **Cadence:** Post into the group 3–5x/week; respond to every comment within 2 hours to maximize algorithmic reach

#### Facebook Live

Live video is experiencing a resurgence driven by Facebook's algorithm rewarding real-time engagement:
- **Best uses for real estate:** weekly market Q+A ("Ask me anything about the Bend market"), open house walk-throughs, just-listed reveals, community event coverage
- **Announce 48 hours ahead** via a regular post; the anticipation post drives reach for the live itself
- **Replay matters:** Post the replay immediately after going live; clips from the replay perform as normal Reels
- **Length:** 15–30 minutes is the engagement sweet spot; longer is better than shorter because dwell time is the primary signal
- **Branding:** Name lower-third is standard and expected in FB Live; no penalty for visible branding in live format

#### Branding Pattern — Facebook Tolerates More

Facebook's audience is older, more accustomed to explicit branding, and less likely to penalize it algorithmically. Evidence:

- Ryan Serhant's SERHANT. brand appears on every listing post without engagement drop-off
- Listing post engagement rates are comparable with or without visible logos/branding on FB (unlike TikTok where branding tanks organic reach)
- **Practical guidance:** Agent name + "Ryan Realty" + phone in a lower-third on listing Reels is acceptable on FB. The same watermark on TikTok would suppress reach 30–50%.
- **Logo in paid ads:** Standard practice; credibility-building; expected by the audience. A real estate ad without branding feels untrustworthy to the 45–65 demographic
- **Matt's face on camera:** Yes, and more than IG. FB's trust model is relationship-based; the face builds the relationship. 1–2 face-to-camera posts per week on FB generates more lead conversions than polished graphic content alone

#### Caption Length + CTA

- **Organic posts:** 50–150 characters for maximum reach; tease, don't explain
- **Paid lead ad copy:** 3-line structure works best — (1) pattern interrupt/stat, (2) benefit statement, (3) specific CTA
- **CTA buttons:** "Learn More" and "Schedule Now" outperform "Contact Us" by measurable margins in real estate vertical; "Get Quote" works for home valuation offers
- **Posting cadence:** 5x/week for an active presence (2 Reels, 2 image/carousel posts, 1 text/opinion post); minimum 3x/week to maintain algorithmic presence

### Paid Lead-Gen Creative Best Practices

This is the most important section for Ryan Realty's Bend market. Facebook is the strongest paid platform for residential real estate — 35% of all Meta Lead Ads are real estate, the highest of any industry category.

#### Ad Creative Formula — The Three-Act Structure

Every high-converting real estate Facebook lead ad follows this structure:

**Act 1 — Pattern Interrupt (hook, first 3 seconds / first line of copy)**
- Specific market stat: "Bend home prices up 8.3% YoY — but inventory just spiked."
- Contrarian claim: "Most buyers in Bend are still waiting. Here's why that's a mistake."
- Local data: "3 homes in NW Crossing closed above list price last week."
- Avoid: generic questions ("Looking to buy?"), claims that could be said about any market

**Act 2 — Value Proposition (what they get)**
- Lead magnets that convert for real estate: neighborhood market reports, instant home valuation, buyer's guide for the specific area, school district comparison, relocation guide
- The offer must be specific to Central Oregon, not generic. "Bend Market Report — Q2 2026" outperforms "Free Real Estate Guide" by significant margin
- Frame for the buyer, not the agent: "See what your Bend home is worth today" not "We provide free home valuations"

**Act 3 — CTA (what to do next)**
- "Get my report" / "See my estimate" / "Download the guide"
- "Learn More" and "Schedule Now" outperform "Contact Us" in real estate
- Avoid "Submit" — it implies form submission friction

#### Lead Form Optimization

- **3–5 fields maximum.** Each field above 5 drops completion rates 5–15%.
- **Recommended structure:** Name (auto-filled), Email (auto-filled), Phone (auto-filled), + 1 qualifying question ("Are you looking to buy or sell within the next 12 months?")
- **Higher Intent toggle:** Enable "Higher Intent" in Meta Lead Ad settings — adds a confirmation step, reduces volume 40%, but increases qualified lead quality 2–3x. For a principal broker with a high commission floor, quality > volume.
- **First contact within 5 minutes is critical:** Leads contacted in under 5 minutes convert at 21x the rate of those contacted after 30 minutes. Automate FUB or a similar CRM to trigger an SMS + email the moment a form is submitted.

#### Image vs. Video

- **Video outperforms static in every real estate category** — 2.2x engagement vs. static images for short-form video under 30 seconds
- **UGC/authentic > polished:** "A selfie-style testimonial or agent-on-location video from the work site outperforms a $5,000 studio-shot ad in 9 out of 10 accounts" (LeadSync 2026 data). This maps to Matt filming a 20-second market take on his phone > a produced listing walk-through for ad purposes.
- **Carousel:** Strong for listing showcases (exterior → kitchen → living → master → yard). 2–10 cards. Headline per card keeps people swiping.
- **Static image:** Still works for home valuation offers with a clean visual + bold headline. Lower CPL but lower click quality.

#### Headline Patterns That Convert

- "[City] Home Prices Are [Direction]. Here's What It Means for You."
- "What's Your [Neighborhood] Home Worth in [Month Year]?"
- "[Number] Homes in [Zip] Sold in [Timeframe]. Is Yours Next?"
- "Thinking of Moving to Bend? Read This First."
- Headline character limit: 27 characters for Feed; 55 for Reels/Stories — keep it punchy

#### Audience Targeting Principles

- **Geographic:** Tight radius (15 miles for listing-specific; broader for brand awareness and relocation buyers)
- **Interest stacking:** Real estate website visitors (Zillow, Trulia), home improvement interest, "likely to move" behavioral segment
- **Lookalike audiences:** Build from closed clients in FUB, not from form fills. Closed-client lookalikes produce 20–40% better ROI than form-fill lookalikes.
- **Advantage+ Audiences:** Meta's automated targeting now outperforms most manual setups; use it with a tight geographic anchor and let the system optimize. Relocation buyer campaigns benefit from broader geographic targeting (Bay Area → Bend campaigns).
- **Conversions API (CAPI):** Critical for recovering iOS-blocked conversion data. Sends hashed server-side signals from FUB/CRM directly to Meta. Expect 15–30% lower CPL with CAPI properly configured.
- **Campaign budget split:** Awareness 30–40% / Consideration 40–45% / Conversion 25–30%

### Winning Patterns

#### Native Video vs. Document Posts vs. Text — 2026 Performance Data

| Format | Avg. Engagement Rate | YoY Change | Algorithm Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Document/Carousel (PDF)** | 6.6–7.0% | +14% YoY | Highest | Dwell time 3–4x longer than text; highest algorithmic reward |
| **Native Video** | 5.6% | +36% YoY | High and rising | Videos under 30s achieve 200% higher completion; 2x more engagement than text |
| **Text-only** | ~2.0–2.8% | Flat | Moderate | Works when the insight is genuinely strong; first-mover advantage on breaking news |
| **External links** | ~1.2% (suppressed) | Declining | Penalized | 60% reach reduction; put links in first comment |

**2026 verdict:** Lead with document carousels + native video. Use text posts for real-time market takes when you're first with a data point. Never put links in post body.

#### Hook — First Line Is Everything

LinkedIn posts show only the first ~210 characters before "See more." The hook must either stop the scroll or lose the reader entirely.

Proven hook structures for real estate/relocation content:

- **Contrarian data point:** "Everyone says the Bend market is cooling. The last 30 days say otherwise."
- **Specific number:** "We closed 14 listings in Q1 2026. Here's what surprised us."
- **Confession/lesson:** "I priced a listing wrong last year. Here's what I'd do differently."
- **Prediction with reasoning:** "Bend's rental vacancy rate tells me where purchase demand is going in Q3."
- **Relocating buyer address:** "You're a tech lead in Seattle thinking about Bend. Here are the three questions you actually need answered."

Avoid: generic questions, exclamation marks, emojis (per brand rules), and anything a thousand other agents could say.

#### Length Sweet Spot

LinkedIn rewards **longer** captions than Instagram — but structure is the multiplier. The data:
- **150–250 words** is the sweet spot for personal profiles (up to 3,000 char limit)
- **Company page posts:** 700 char maximum — requires even tighter editing
- **Paragraphs of 1–3 lines max** with white space between; wall of text = scroll-past
- "See more" click is a positive signal to the algorithm; write hooks that earn it
- Short paragraphs + white space = 40% higher read-through rate vs. dense blocks

#### Engagement Formula — Comments Beat Likes

LinkedIn's algorithm weighs comments significantly more than likes. The implication:

1. **Post a question or genuine debate prompt** in every post — not engagement bait, but an actual open question from the post's insight
2. **Respond to every comment within the first 60 minutes** — this is the critical distribution window; posts that receive early comments get pushed to a wider audience
3. **Comment strategically on large accounts** ("drafting off giants," per Justin Welsh) — add a genuine insight to a relevant post from a 100K+ account within 15 minutes of their posting; top comment visibility yields 100K+ impressions for your profile
4. **Engage 10–15 posts per day** from peers and potential referral partners; systematic commenting builds referral relationships faster than direct outreach

#### Posting Cadence — Less Is More

- **2–3 posts per week** (personal profile); never post daily — quality dilutes and audience fatigues
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best time:** 8–10 AM in your audience's timezone (Bay Area tech workers = Pacific time = matches Bend)
- **Company page:** 3–5x/week maximum; quality over frequency
- Plan on 6–9 months before meaningful referral flow; compounding begins around month 9

#### Hashtag Strategy — Changed in Late 2024

LinkedIn removed hashtag following in late 2024. Hashtags now function as **categorization signals** to the 360Brew AI model, not discovery tools.
- Use **3–5 hashtags maximum** per post
- Mix: 1–2 broad industry tags + 1–2 niche topic tags
- Real estate relevant: `#RealEstate` `#BendOregon` `#CentralOregon` `#Relocation` `#LuxuryRealEstate` `#HousingMarket`
- Avoid giant generic tags (`#Business`, `#Work`); niche drives better-targeted distribution

## Cross-Platform: Where the Two Diverge

| **Trust mechanism** | Relationship + face-on-camera + community | Expertise + data + specificity |
| **Branding tolerance** | High — logo, brokerage name, phone in listing posts is standard and expected | Low — name-forward only; no logo in feed posts; brand = the content quality |
| **Caption length** | 50–150 chars for max organic reach | 150–250 words with structured paragraphs; "See more" is a positive signal |
| **Top format** | Reels (15–30s) for discovery; carousel for engagement | Document/PDF carousel (6.6% engagement rate); native video rising |
| **Links in posts** | In first comment (FB suppresses link posts) | In first comment (LI penalizes with 60% reach reduction) |
| **Paid channel** | Yes — strongest paid lead-gen in residential real estate; 35% of Meta Lead Ads are real estate | Limited — not a meaningful paid lead-gen channel for residential; LI ads are expensive and built for B2B SaaS |
| **Hashtags** | Not relevant for organic reach | 3–5 for topic categorization signal to algorithm |
| **Posting cadence** | 3–5x/week for organic; daily posting with ad spend is fine | 2–3x/week; quality >> frequency; daily = dilution |
| **What bombs** | Long text-only posts (suppressed); content without a clear local anchor; high-production content that looks like an ad | Promotional listing posts ("Just Listed!"); overly branded content; posts with external links in body; posting frequency > 5x/week |
| **Group strategy** | Core organic play; community groups generate real estate leads at very low cost | N/A — LinkedIn Groups are largely dead; focus is on personal feed + commenting on others |
| **Face-on-camera** | Critical for trust + conversion in this demographic | Effective but not essential; the writing can carry without video |
| **Audience discovery** | Interest-based algorithm (like TikTok) — non-followers can find you | Primarily network-based + dwell time signals; discovery is slower |

**What works on FB that bombs on LI:**
- "Just Listed" posts with address + price + "DM for details" → works on FB; feels spammy on LI
- High-energy community group engagement → meaningful on FB; LI Groups are vestigial
- Facebook Live open houses → zero LI equivalent; LI Live is rarely used for real estate
- Aggressive retargeting with your face everywhere → acceptable on FB; feels invasive on LI

**What works on LI that bombs on FB:**
- 200-word market analysis post with no visuals → high engagement on LI from professional audience; ignored on FB feed
- LinkedIn Newsletter (30–50% open rate) → no equivalent on FB
- Deal storytelling with nuanced deal economics → sophisticated LI audience loves it; FB audience wants the short take
- "Drafting off giants" (commenting on industry leaders' posts) → significant distribution on LI; no equivalent mechanism on FB
