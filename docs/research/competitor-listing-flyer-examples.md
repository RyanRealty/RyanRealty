## 2. Typical single-property listing post — pattern by account

Almost universal across the list:

- **Carousel beats single image.** 6–10 slides. Slide 1 is the hero. Slides 2–8 are the interior progression (kitchen, primary suite, view, outdoor). Final slide is a contact / agent card.
- **Full-bleed photo.** No borders, no frames, no margins. Photo runs to all four edges of the 1080×1080 (or 1080×1350) canvas.
- **Price is usually hidden on the hero card.** Luxury accounts (Joyce Rey, Branden Williams, The Agency, Aaron Kirman) default to "Price Upon Request" or no price at all. Price shows in the caption, not the image. Mid-luxury (Ladd Group, Cascade SIR, Chad Carroll) does show price, but only on one slide late in the carousel — not the hero.
- **Address lives at the bottom 15–20% of the photo,** either over a subtle dark scrim or in the lower-third caption zone. Never centered. Never giant. Usually a single line: *"237 W Tumalo Reservoir Rd · Bend, OR"*.
- **Logo presence on hero slide is minimal or zero.** A small monogram in a corner at most. The brokerage mark goes on the final agent card, not the property hero. SERHANT, Compass, and Sotheby's accounts all do this — brand is the type system + color, not a slapped-on logo.
- **Agent face is on the final slide only.** None of the top accounts put the agent's headshot on the hero. The hero is the property. The agent appears on slide 8 or 9 as a sign-off card.
- **Captions are story-led.** "Wake up to the sound of the Deschutes" — not "4 bed, 3 bath, 3,200 sqft, MLS#220189422." Specs go below the fold or in the carousel, never in the lead caption.

### Type register, by account cluster

- **Sotheby's-affiliated (Cascade SIR, Ladd Group, Joyce Rey when at Coldwell Global Luxury):** Minion serif for numbers, Gill Sans for addresses. SIR blue (#002349-ish navy). Restrained, classical.
- **Compass-affiliated (Chad Carroll, Tracy Tutor, Madison Sutton via SERHANT-Compass crossover):** Black/white only. Compass enforces single-color reproduction. Sans-serif throughout (Compass Sans / Inter-adjacent). Very flat, very modern.
- **The Agency / Williams & Williams / Hilton & Hyland register:** Black wordmark, condensed sans for prices, serif (Didot/Bodoni-adjacent) for the address — high editorial register.
- **The Boutique RE Group:** Custom per-listing graphic system. Each listing gets a small bespoke logotype + ribbon treatment. Visually expensive, hard to replicate cheaply.
- **SERHANT / Ryan Serhant:** Bold uppercase grotesque (Founders Grotesk-feeling), red accent (#E1251B-ish), high-contrast.

### Color registers, by account cluster

- **Luxury monochrome:** Black + white + one accent (Aaron Kirman, The Agency, Branden & Rayni). Photo carries 95% of the visual weight.
- **Warm cream + navy (heritage luxury):** Sotheby's affiliates, Carrie Wells in Aspen. Soft cream `#faf8f4`-feel backgrounds on the title slides, navy text. Matches what Ryan Realty is already doing.
- **Photo-first muted:** Ladd Group, Cascade SIR — the photo's own color grade IS the palette; overlays are white at 90% opacity over a subtle dark gradient.
- **Bold brand color:** Nob Hill Compass (red), Chad Carroll (red/black), SERHANT (red accent). Used sparingly — one pill or one stat callout per carousel.

## 3. The five repeatable visual patterns

1. **Full-bleed hero + lower-third address block.** Photo edge-to-edge. Bottom 18–22% is either a soft dark gradient or a thin dark scrim. Address in a single line — street name in serif (Playfair, Tiempos, Didot), city/state in smaller all-caps sans below. No price, no specs, no logo. **Best example: The Agency, Joyce Rey, Cascade Hasson SIR.** This is the cleanest, most universal pattern and the one Tumalo should mimic.

2. **Carousel with one branded "title card" slide.** Cream or off-white background, no photo, just the property name or one-line headline in display serif, with a discrete brokerage mark. Used as slide 1 OR as a chapter break between exterior and interior. Magazine cover energy. **Best example: Heider Real Estate, The Boutique RE Group.**

3. **Hero photo + single dramatic stat overlay.** One number — square footage, acres, elevation, view-feet — set in oversized serif on top of the photo. No address, no other text. The number IS the hook. Works because it pattern-interrupts the "I'm scrolling listings" feed. **Best example: Aaron Kirman, Ben Bacal.**

4. **Split panel — photo left, type right (1080×1080 only, not vertical).** Half the frame is the hero photo, half is a cream/navy block with the address in serif + 3–4 stats stacked in tabular sans. Looks like a real estate magazine spread. **Best example: Tomer Fridman, Williams & Williams.**

5. **Editorial banner — narrow horizontal photo strip on top, deep cream block below with headline + address.** Reverses the lower-third pattern. Works well for wide drone shots that don't fill a 1:1 frame. **Best example: Carrie Wells (Aspen seasonal series), Ladd Group.**

6. **Status pill over hero.** A small "JUST LISTED" / "COMING SOON" / "SOLD" pill in a brand color at the top-left or top-right of the hero photo. Functions as a verbal hook in the visual layer. **Best example: Chad Carroll (his red pill is signature), SERHANT.** Use sparingly — overused, this becomes amateur.

## 4. What makes flyers FAIL — what the top accounts never do

1. **Cluttered text blocks. Specs everywhere.** Amateur MLS-generated flyers cram every number — sqft, lot size, year built, taxes, HOA — onto the hero. Top accounts show 0–1 stats on the hero, and the rest of the data lives in the caption or later carousel slides.
2. **The brokerage logo bigger than the address.** A 200px brokerage logo at the top with a 24px address below is the #1 amateur tell. Top luxury accounts shrink the brokerage mark to a corner monogram or move it entirely to the final card.
3. **Agent face on every slide.** The "headshot + name + phone + email" sidebar on the hero photo. Top accounts treat the agent card as a sign-off slide, not a constant frame.
4. **Default Canva/MLS color schemes.** Bright primary blues, yellows, reds. Drop shadows. Gradient banners. Beveled buttons. Times New Roman. Calibri. Comic Sans for "personality." Anything that looks like a 2007 PowerPoint.
5. **Photo inside a frame with a colored border.** Top accounts run photos full-bleed. The moment a photo sits inside a 40px border with rounded corners and a drop-shadow, it reads as 2014 Postlets/Realtor.com export.
6. **Centered everything.** Centered address. Centered price. Centered logo. Centered phone. Visually static, no hierarchy, no edge tension. Top accounts use left-alignment or bottom-left/bottom-right anchoring.
7. **Stock real estate clip art** — house silhouettes, key icons, "FOR SALE" sign vectors, sold-stamp graphics. Zero in the top-account feeds.

## 5. Best-fit account for Ryan Realty (Tumalo / Bend mountain market)

**Closest fit: Cascade Hasson Sotheby's International Realty (@cascade_sir) and The Ladd Group (@bendpropertysource).**

They're literally in the same market (Bend, Central Oregon), they hit the same luxury-mountain price band, and their visual register is heritage-cream + navy with restrained serif typography — which is exactly what Ryan Realty's v2 design system (navy `#102742` + cream `#faf8f4` + Amboqia/Geist) was built for. Mimic their:

- Full-bleed drone exterior hero on slide 1
- Address in serif bottom-left, city/state in small caps below, no price in frame
- 6–8 interior slides progressing exterior → kitchen → primary → view
- One discrete navy "title card" slide between exterior and interior sections
- Agent card as the final slide, never on the hero

**Stretch reference: The Agency (@theagencyre)** for the editorial register — taking the cream-and-navy base and pushing it more magazine-like, with a single line of serif type on the hero and the rest of the data living in the carousel and caption.

## 7. Quick checklist before shipping a Tumalo flyer

- [ ] Hero is a single drone exterior. Full-bleed. No border, no frame.
- [ ] Address is one line, bottom-left, serif. City/state in small caps below.
- [ ] No price on the hero. Price (and full specs) live in caption + later slides.
- [ ] No agent headshot on the hero. Agent card is the final slide.
- [ ] Brokerage mark is a small navy monogram in a corner, or absent until the final card.
- [ ] Carousel runs 6–10 slides: exterior → optional title card → kitchen → primary → view → outdoor → agent card.
- [ ] No bevels, drop shadows, default Canva color schemes, real-estate clip art, "FOR SALE" stamps.
- [ ] One typography register only: Amboqia for the headline/address, Geist for stats. No third font.
- [ ] Caption is story-led, not spec-led. Specs in a clean stacked list below.
- [ ] If a status pill is used (JUST LISTED), it's small, navy on cream or cream on navy — never bright red.
