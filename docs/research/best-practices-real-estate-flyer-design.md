# Real Estate Flyer Design Best Practices — 2026

Research synthesized from Canva, Etsy top sellers, Design Shack, OKZest, and top Instagram real estate accounts.

## Hero Photo Treatment

**Full-bleed wins.** The top-performing Canva and Etsy templates all lead with a single full-bleed hero photo occupying 55–75% of the flyer surface. Constrained photo boxes (small framed photos inside a border) look cheap and small-screen — the photo IS the flyer for scroll-stopping scroll behavior. Photo grids (contact sheets) are the exception: 6–9 tight thumbnails work only for the "whole property tour" layout type.

Positioning: Photo typically runs top-to-bottom on the left half, or top-60% of the frame, with type landing on a cream/white block below — OR type floats on a dark scrim over the lower third of the image. **Never a full-bleed dark overlay across the whole photo.** The photo needs to breathe.

## Type Hierarchy — Proven Scale

Top templates use a 5-level hierarchy:

1. **Display headline** (Amboqia Boriango or similar serif): 72–100px. Used for the street address, a single dominant stat, or a lifestyle phrase. One line only. Tracked tight (−0.01em).
2. **Price line** (Geist or bold sans): 52–68px. Price always formatted with comma: `$1,225,000`. Never smaller than 48px.
3. **Spec bar** (Geist 500, all-caps, tracked 0.10em): 20–26px. BD · BA · SQFT · ACRES as a single horizontal line with middle-dot separators.
4. **Body/narrative** (Geist 400): 16–20px, max 3 lines. Lifestyle pitch, not a spec sheet.
5. **Contact line** (Geist 400): 14–16px. Name · phone · web · DRE # if required.

## Information Architecture — What Goes Where

The pattern that dominates Canva's top 20 and Etsy best-sellers:

```
[HERO PHOTO — full bleed, 55-70% of frame]
[ADDRESS — display serif, large]
[PRICE + SPEC BAR]
[1–3 sentence narrative]
[LOGO + AGENT CONTACT]
```

The key insight: **weight flows top to bottom, with photo as anchor.** Amateur flyers front-load text and bury the photo. Pro flyers front-load the photo and let the text layer below it.

For broker-branded flyers (F8 Track Record), the agent portrait moves to a large anchoring position, and the stats become the "photo."

## White Space — The Most Violated Rule

Pro real estate flyers use **18–28% white space.** This feels like a lot. Most amateur flyers pack every pixel. The padding on pro flyers:

- Margin: 40–60px on all sides at 1080px width
- Gutters between text blocks: 16–24px minimum
- Above/below spec bar: 12px breathing room with a 1px divider

Rule of thumb: if you can fit one more line, don't.

## Decorative Elements Within Brand

The elements that appear most on top-selling Etsy templates and Canva's featured picks:

- **Fine rules / dividers**: 1px lines in navy or 15% opacity black, separating spec bar from body
- **Middle-dot separators**: `·` in spec bars (3 BD · 3 BA · 2,325 SQFT)
- **All-caps eyebrow labels**: `TUMALO, OREGON` in Azo Sans Medium, tracked 0.12em, above the address
- **Geographic accent**: subtle coordinate text `44.0321° N  121.3138° W` as a watermark detail
- **Thin outer border**: 1px navy or 1px cream border inset 8px from the edge on cream-background flyers

No gradients. No drop shadows on text boxes. No rounded photo containers. No geometric shapes floating randomly.

## Content Density — Less Is More

The scroll-stopping formula: **one dominant visual question** answered by one dominant text element.

- F1 Museum Wall: "What does this place look like?" → Full-bleed photo answers it.
- F3 Stat Spike: "What's the defining number?" → One giant number answers it.
- F9 Open House: "When can I see it?" → Date/time dominates.

Top Instagram real estate accounts (@serhant, @theagencyre) routinely post flyers with:
- 1 photo
- 1 headline (address or lifestyle phrase)
- 1 price
- 1 spec line
- 1 contact line

That's 5 elements total. Everything else is removed.

## Photo Overlays — Scrim Discipline

Dark scrim rules from the top templates:
- Scrim only where text must land, not full-bleed
- Max opacity: `rgba(0,0,0,0.45)` — photo still visible through it
- Hard rectangle, not gradient (gradients look like old-school 2015 real estate)
- Bottom-third scrim for price/address overlay = the dominant pattern

## 2026 Dominant Aesthetic

The clearest trend: **editorial minimalism.** Magazine-quality photography paired with serif display type and a restrained sans for data. Navy/cream color duos (or navy/white) are beating out the gold-accent look. Busy templates with many colors, many sections, and decorative backgrounds are declining. One photo. One message. One brand.
