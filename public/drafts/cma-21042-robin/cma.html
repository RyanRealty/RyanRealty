Why this matters

Buyers in the $1M–$1.2M Tumalo tier are paying a clear premium for renovated, move-in-ready homes on 2–3 acres with views. The improvements completed since 2022 pull this house out of the "2000-build, dated finishes" comp bucket and into the renovated turnkey tier — which is exactly where the most recent subdivision comps cleared north of $440/sqft.

The narrative for pricing isn't 2,592 sqft × subdivision average. It's 2,592 sqft × renovated-condition multiplier, anchored on the two 2026 sales at 65258 Old Bend Redmond ($1.20M) and 65299 85th ($1.135M, new build).

One unresolved item — the deck

The deck has been sanded and stained twice and took some damage from the roofers. A full TREX replacement is estimated at ~$40,000. Recovery on a TREX deck in this tier is typically 50–60%, so the math is a ~$20–24K value-add for $40K out of pocket — but the more important effect is on photos and showings. A clean deck at this price point materially affects how the home reads in the first 90 seconds online. Recommendation: replace before listing if cash flow allows; if not, price into the lower half of the range and disclose.

What the comps actually say

  • Two pricing tiers are visible inside Whispering Pines. Updated/turnkey homes are clearing $411–$462/sqft. Lightly-updated or dated homes are clearing $284–$340/sqft. The spread is real — the subdivision rewards renovation.
  • 65258 Old Bend Redmond is the cleanest anchor. 2,599 sqft (within 0.3% of subject), 2.50 acres, 2004 build, closed Feb 2026 at full list of $1.2M in 44 days. This is the single best read on what a renovated, well-positioned home on this stretch is worth in today's market.
  • 65195 85th sets the un-renovated floor. 3,020 sqft, 2.79 acres, same 2000 build year as the subject, sold Feb 2025 at $940K / $311/sqft — almost certainly with original or lightly-updated finishes given the price per foot.
  • The new-build at 65299 85th sets a soft ceiling. Brand-new 2025 construction at $441/sqft tells us a renovated 2000-build will sit just below that — not above it.
  • Days on market vary widely. The two recent < 50 DOM sales (65258 OBR, 65195 85th) both went at or near list with clean photos and no significant condition issues. Long-DOM sales in the data set were priced above market entry and adjusted late.

Pricing Strategy

Two methods, one answer. Both bracket the subject at roughly the same place — which gives us a defensible range.

Method 1 — Tiered price-per-sqft (renovated bracket)

Apply the renovated/turnkey $/sqft range observed in the subdivision ($410–$460/sqft) to the subject's 2,592 sqft. The post-improvement profile of the subject puts it squarely in this tier, not the un-renovated $284–$340 tier.

$410 × 2,592 = $1,062,720   |   $460 × 2,592 = $1,192,320

Method 2 — Un-renovated baseline + improvement value-add

Where the 2022 listing went wrong

The 2022 list at $1,195,000 expired. Two reasons. First, in mid-2022 the broader Bend market was already cooling from its 2021 peak and high-end Tumalo inventory was building. Second, the home at that time did not have the renovation that justifies a $400+/sqft price tag — and the photos still online from that period reflect the pre-renovation finishes. Today the price is supportable; in 2022 it was not. The fix is two-fold: re-shoot the photos to reflect the current condition (the existing online imagery is the single biggest risk to getting a buyer through the door), and price at the recommended tier rather than the stretch tier unless the deck issue is resolved first.
