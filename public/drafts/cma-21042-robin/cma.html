Why this matters

Buyers in the $1M–$1.2M Tumalo tier are paying a clear premium for renovated, move-in-ready homes on 2–3 acres with views. The improvements completed since 2022 pull this house out of the "2000-build, dated finishes" comp bucket and into the renovated turnkey tier — which is exactly where the most recent subdivision comps cleared north of $440/sqft.

The narrative for pricing is not 2,592 sqft × subdivision average. It is 2,592 sqft × renovated-condition multiplier, anchored on the two 2026 sales at 65258 Old Bend Redmond ($1.20M) and 65299 85th ($1.135M, new build).

One unresolved item — the deck

The deck has been sanded and stained twice and took some damage from the roofers. A full TREX replacement is estimated at ~$40,000. Recovery on a TREX deck in this tier is typically 50–60%, so the math is a ~$20–24K value-add for $40K out of pocket — but the more important effect is on photos and showings. A clean deck at this price point materially affects how the home reads in the first 90 seconds online. Recommendation: replace before listing if cash flow allows; if not, price in the lower half of the range and disclose.

Pricing Strategy

Two methods, one answer. Both bracket the subject at roughly the same place — that's the math check.

Method 1 — Tiered price-per-sqft (renovated bracket)

Apply the renovated/turnkey $/sqft range observed in the subdivision ($410–$460/sqft) to the subject's 2,592 sqft. The post-improvement profile puts the subject squarely in this tier, not the un-renovated $284–$340 tier.

$410 × 2,592 = $1,062,720   |   $460 × 2,592 = $1,192,320

Method 2 — Un-renovated baseline + improvement value-add

Start from the un-renovated subdivision baseline of $940K–$975K (anchored on 65195 85th and 65318 85th, both ~2000 builds at full sqft equivalent), then add the realistic improvement value-add of $177K–$213K from page 2.

$940,000 + $177,000 = $1,117,000   |   $975,000 + $213,000 = $1,188,000

Where the 2022 listing went wrong

The 2022 list at $1,195,000 expired. Two reasons. First, by mid-2022 the broader Bend market was already cooling from its 2021 peak and high-end Tumalo inventory was building. Second, the home at that time did not have the renovation that justifies a $400+/sqft price tag, and the photos still online from that period reflect the pre-renovation finishes. Today the price is supportable; in 2022 it was not. The fix is two-fold: re-shoot the photos to reflect the current condition (the existing online imagery is the single biggest risk to getting a buyer through the door), and price at the recommended tier rather than the stretch tier unless the deck is resolved first.
