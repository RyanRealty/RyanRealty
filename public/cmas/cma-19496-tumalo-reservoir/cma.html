Why this matters

Rural Tumalo acreage between Bend and Redmond is its own market — buyers in the $1M–$1.6M tier are paying for view exposure, lot privacy, and condition. There is no formal subdivision filter to anchor on. The comp set on page 4 is built by distance (≤ 3 miles), property class (SFR with acreage), and a tight size band (1,700–2,900 sqft, 0.76–6.84 acres, vintage 1980–2010).

The narrative for pricing is view + lot quality + condition tier, not raw $/sqft. The closest comparable on lifestyle and lot (65010 Gerking Market) closed at $1.20M in Jan 2026. The strongest view + renovation comp (64141 Tumalo Rim) closed $125K above list at $1.60M in Aug 2025.

What the listing history tells us

The current Active position is defensible against renovated subdivision comps in the 2-acre tier and below the view-premium ceiling — the right question now is whether to hold the line, drop incrementally, or sharpen the marketing presentation.

Pricing Strategy

Two methods, one answer. Both bracket the subject at roughly the same place — that is the math check.

Method 1 — Tiered price-per-sqft (middle bracket)

The five comps sort into three tiers by $/sqft and condition. The renovated view ceiling sits alone at $766/sqft (Tumalo Rim). The middle tier — same vintage, mountain views, marketed at asking on a 1–3 acre rural lot — clears $470–$540/sqft (Gerking Market $497, Saddleback $530, Gold Spur $427). Apply the middle bracket to the subject's 2,325 sqft. The subject's larger lot, water feature, heated 3-car garage, and Three Sisters orientation place it toward the upper end of this band.

$470 × 2,325 = $1,092,750   |   $540 × 2,325 = $1,255,500

Method 2 — Closest comparable + lot & view premium

Anchor on 65010 Gerking Market: $1,200,000 in Jan 2026 for 2,416 sqft on 5 acres at $497/sqft, view-equivalent to the subject. The subject is 91 sqft smaller (-3.8%) and on a smaller lot (-2.7 acres) — net comp-equivalent floor of roughly $1,100,000. Add a Three Sisters direct-view premium (~$80–$125K) and credit the heated 3-car garage + water feature + RV parking package (~$25–$50K). Ceiling tested against the renovated view comp at $1,600,000 (Tumalo Rim) — the subject is not full-reno, so it sits below.

$1,100,000 + $80,000 + $25,000 = $1,205,000   |   $1,100,000 + $125,000 + $50,000 = $1,275,000

Why this list price

Reading the 64141 Tumalo Rim outlier

The renovated view comp at 64141 Tumalo Rim closed $125,000 over its $1,475,000 list price in Aug 2025 — a $766/sqft transaction in a market where the middle of the comp set sits at $470–$540/sqft. The right read is not "the subject can clear $1.6M." The right read is "a full renovation with a 500 sqft addition on a Tumalo lot with Cascade views can compress to 8 days under contract and push 15% above list." The subject's 1995 build is not in the same condition tier; its ceiling is below the Tumalo Rim transaction. The Tumalo Rim closing instead tells us the demand for renovated view product is real — operationally useful if a future seller decides to invest in updates before a relist.

Why holding $1,225,000 makes sense

The prior list at $1,449,000 sat 492 days without producing a qualifying offer — a 17% over-shoot of the eventual comp evidence. The current $1,225,000 list is 15.5% below that prior position and sits within the converged range from both methods on the prior page. With 62 active days on market, the data does not yet say "drop the price." It says "hold the line, refresh the marketing, and reassess at 90 active days." The first meaningful inflection point is at 90 days — if a qualifying offer has not surfaced by then, the next move is a measured drop into the $1,175,000–$1,199,000 band rather than a panic cut.
