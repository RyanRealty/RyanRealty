What top brokerages actually post for listings

Pulled 52 recent posts from Hilton & Hyland, The Agency, Douglas Elliman, and Ryan Serhant via Apify. Filtered to single-property listing posts. Here's what they're doing — primary source, not described, shown.

Pattern A — “the photo IS the post”

Unaltered photograph. No overlay. No logo. No spec line.

The most common pattern across Hilton & Hyland, Douglas Elliman, and ~70% of The Agency's listings. The professionally-shot property photo carries the entire post. Every overlay element — price, BR/BA, address, “JUST LISTED” badge, brokerage logo, agent headshot — lives in the caption underneath the post, not on top of it.

Pattern B — “magazine cover” (much less common, ~20% of posts)

Single editorial headline overlaid on a quiet portion of the hero photo.

Used by The Agency primarily. Serif white type. Selective italicization for emphasis on a couple of words (property style, location). Price stated conversationally (“for $19M”), never as a stat-line. Photo crop is intentional — the headline lands in a sky region, a dark ceiling, or another quiet area so it doesn't compete with the architecture. Photo still dominates ~75% of the frame.

What this means for the Tumalo listing

Every “flyer” we tried to build was the wrong category. Top brokerages don't post listing flyers. They post unedited professional photographs — one photo per post or as a carousel — with a story-led caption that does the heavy lifting that we kept trying to bake into the image.

What we should ship

  • 10 unaltered listing photos as IG carousel slides. Color-corrected, retouched for clarity, but no overlay. No logo. No spec line. No JUST LISTED sticker. The photos already exist in the Spark CDN feed.
  • One H&H-style listing caption, written in your voice, 250–400 words, location-anchored opening, materials/architectural details middle, lifestyle closing, structured property-details block at the bottom (》address 》price 》BR/BA 》sqft).
  • One optional Agency-style “magazine cover” variant — only on the strongest hero photo, serif italic-emphasis headline placed in the sky portion. Used as slide 1 of the carousel OR as a single-image announcement post. Built in Canva natively or as a clean Remotion comp.
  • An optional 4–6 page Cascade-Hasson-style lookbook PDF, served from the Tumalo listing page on ryan-realty.com. Editorial register: cream/white, restrained serif headline, photography spreads with single-line captions, agent close.

What we should NOT do (every top brokerage avoids these)

  • never Brokerage logo on the image
  • never Agent headshot on the image
  • never Spec line baked into the image (BD · BA · SF · acres as overlay)
  • never Phone / email / web address on the image
  • never Open-house dates on the image (those go in stories, not posts)
  • never “JUST LISTED” stickers/badges over the photo
  • never Colored panels, borders, or frames around the photo
  • never Multi-photo collages (use carousels instead — one photo per slide)
