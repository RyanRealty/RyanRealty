# Platform Best Practices.  Ryan Realty 2026

## The single most important rule

**The logo is a closer, not an opener.**

Every short-form platform.  TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Facebook Reels.  trains its algorithm to suppress content that looks like an ad. An in-frame logo in the first two seconds is the clearest possible ad signal. The algorithm interprets it as promotional content and reduces distribution to non-followers. The top real estate creators (Glennda Baker at 350K+ daily views, Daniel Heider at 3.7M TikTok followers, Madison Sutton with 100% of her business sourced from social) run zero in-frame logos on short-form organic content. Brand recognition is built through a consistent visual system.  color palette, voice, face, typography.  not through a stamped wordmark. The logo earns its placement by appearing only where viewers expect a credential: end card, thumbnail, print, carousel final slide, email header, yard sign. The Chanel parallel is exact: over-exposure of a logo devalues it. A SERHANT. bug on every frame makes it an ad. Restraint IS the luxury signal.

This applies to Ryan Realty: Matt's face + the Bend landscape + the navy/cream palette + a consistent voice, repeated across 30+ pieces of content, builds more brand equity than a logo watermark on every frame.

## Per-platform deep dive

### Instagram (Reels + Carousel + Feed + Story)

**Algorithm signals 2026** (per TrueFuture Media, Buffer 9.6M post analysis): saves > shares > comments > likes. Design every piece of content to earn saves and DM shares.  those are the signals that push content to non-followers. A carousel with 500 saves beats a Reel with 5,000 likes algorithmically.

**Hook formula:** Motion must exist by frame 12 (0.4s). Content text must appear by frame 30 (1.0s). 60%+ three-second hold rate is the distribution threshold.  content below 40% is algorithmically suppressed. Banned openings: logo on black, slow boundary pan, "Hey guys welcome back," wide drone with no overlay.

**Length sweet spots:** Reels: 15-30s for discovery; 30-60s for educational/narrative. Carousels: 7-10 slides (algorithm re-serves carousels to users who didn't complete all slides.  unique re-engagement feature). Stories: 15s clips, daily.

**Caption pattern:**
1. First line = the hook (most people never click "more"; lead with the specific fact, not the preamble)
2. Location name early (city/neighborhood → Instagram search discovery)
3. Question close with a short answer ("Which of these would you choose?" "Buying before or after summer?")
4. ManyChat CTA: "Comment BEND below and I'll DM you our relocation guide".  keyword-reply automation captures warm leads and boosts comment count (algorithm signal). One documented campaign generated 340 qualified leads in 48 hours.

**The ManyChat comment-keyword-DM loop is a required pattern, not optional.** It is the highest-converting native IG lead-gen mechanic in 2026. Set up the automation before publishing any Reel with a CTA.

**Logo restraint:** No logo in Reel frames.  per Glennda Baker (zero in-frame branding, 350K+ daily views), Madison Sutton (near-zero, 100% TikTok/IG client sourcing), and the platform's confirmed suppression of watermarked-feeling content. Carousel: logo on final slide only. Feed graphic posts: subtle footer zone, bottom 10% of image.

**Posting cadence + best times (Buffer, 9.6M posts):**
- Thursday 9 AM: highest single-time reach
- Wednesday/Thursday 12-2 PM: strong secondary
- Weekday evenings 5-7 PM: listing reveal window (post-work house-hunting)
- 10 PM-12 AM: highest Reel view count
- Avoid Friday-Sunday for discovery posts

**Content mix (40/25/20/15.  the inverse of what most agents post):**
- 40% value/educational
- 25% relatable/personality
- 20% behind-the-scenes
- 15% promotional/listings

### TikTok

**TikTok is a search engine.** 64% of Gen Z and 49% of all US consumers now use TikTok as a primary search tool. The platform indexes: spoken words (speech-to-text), on-screen text (OCR), caption keywords, and hashtags. Write VO scripts as long-tail SEO landing pages.  include the search query verbatim in both VO and on-screen text. If "Bend Oregon homes under $600,000" appears on screen and is spoken, those words are searchable.

**Length sweet spot 2026 (shift from 2024):** The platform moved toward rewarding longer content (60-180s) as it competes with YouTube for watch time. But "longer" only works with relentless pacing.  no dead air.
- Property tours: 20-35s
- Educational/market commentary: 60-90s
- Storytelling/client anecdotes: 60-120s
- Default for Ryan Realty: **30-45s tours, 60-90s market/educational**
- Absolute max: 90s.  beyond this, completion drops below the 70% threshold for most real estate content

**Algorithm signal hierarchy 2026 (highest to lowest):**
1. Rewatch/loop rate.  viewer who watches twice sends a stronger signal than two single-view watchers
2. Completion rate.  must hit 70%+ for algorithmic expansion
3. Shares.  viewer stakes their social currency on your content
4. Saves.  predict future intent; high-authority signal
5. Meaningful comments (words, not just emojis)
6. Follower conversion

**Likes are the lowest-weighted signal.** 500 saves + 1,000 likes outperforms 50 saves + 10,000 likes.

**Trending audio:** Use within 24-48 hours of a sound's emergence for the biggest algorithmic lift. For VO-driven content, trending audio at low background volume preserves the signal without competing with information. Original audio is gaining ground.  TikTok signals a preference, and creator-backed sonic branding can become a recognizable asset.

**Logo restraint.  most aggressive of any platform:** Zero in-frame logos. This is unambiguous. TikTok's developer Content Posting API guidelines explicitly prohibit superimposing brand logos. The culture actively punishes it. The algorithm treats watermark-heavy content as low-quality. **Zero of the top 10 real estate TikTok creators put a brokerage logo on their video frames.** (Verified across Daniel Heider 3.7M followers, Glennda Baker 871K, Madison Sutton, Levi Pinson 1M, Cash Jordan 642K.) Brand identity lives in: consistent color palette in overlays, consistent voice, account handle, and profile image.

**Hashtag strategy 2026:** TikTok de-emphasized hashtags; caption keywords and spoken audio do more work. Use 3-5 max.  1 broad (#realestate), 1-2 niche (#oregonrealestate, #bendoregon), 1 trending if relevant, 1 geographic (#centraloregonahomes). Primary keywords belong in plain language in the first caption line, not as hashtags: "Bend Oregon homes under $700K.  here's what the market looks like right now."

**Bend/Tumalo niche opportunity:** The #bendoregon content space is essentially unclaimed by any consistent, high-quality creator. The geographic algorithm delivers local content to local audiences. Content covering "what $X gets you in Tumalo," "Bend vs. Redmond vs. Sisters," wildfire insurance costs, water rights, and STR rules has genuine search demand and near-zero competition. This is the single highest-ROI TikTok opportunity in the market.

**Posting cadence:** 3-5x/week consistently. Best time: Sunday 9 AM; best window: 6-9 AM local. Respond to all comments within the first hour after posting.  engagement velocity in that window determines initial distribution.

### YouTube.  Shorts + Long-form

These are different sports. Treat them separately.

#### YouTube Shorts

Behaves like TikTok in most respects. The algorithm measures the **swipe rate**.  what percentage of viewers who encountered the Short stayed vs. swiped past. Below 70% hold means the first frame needs to change.

**Vertical 9:16.  always.** Non-9:16 Shorts get black bars and signal low quality. Underperform by 30-40%.

**Length: 25-50s is the real sweet spot.** A 30s Short at 85% watch duration beats a 60s Short at 50% watch duration algorithmically. Ryan Realty default: **30-45s** (matches existing production standard).

**No end screens or cards in Shorts.** No visible watermark in the Shorts feed interface. Logo in first 2 seconds kills CTR. Brand through voice consistency, channel profile picture, and username visible in feed.

**Loop/replay design:** Shorts that loop seamlessly get boosted.  replay counts signal satisfaction. End on the same visual you opened on.

**Shorts-to-long-form funnel:** Post Shorts version of the strongest 30-45s from each long-form the same day. Pin a comment: "Full neighborhood breakdown → link in bio." Channels combining Shorts + long-form grow 41% faster than single-format channels.

#### YouTube long-form

**YouTube is a search engine, not a discovery engine.** The title must contain the exact phrase a buyer types into the search bar. Algorithm rewards consistent weekly publishing over 12+ months more than burst posting.

**Thumbnail formula (most important variable.  ~50% of CTR):**
- One face + one property + maximum 5 words + two-color contrast
- Face in thumbnail achieves 20-38% higher CTR than property-only (confirmed across 500K+ video dataset)
- For listings: hero exterior + price in thumbnail text ("$895,000.  Bend, OR")
- Ryan Realty spec: Matt's face (genuine expression.  not fake surprise), property/geographic landmark in remaining 50-60%, 3-5 words, navy `#102742` band or cream `#faf8f4` text, Amboqia Boriango for headline word, Geist bold for supporting text

**Four title formulas (ranked by lead conversion):**
1. "Moving to [City], [State].  [Number] Things to Know ([Year])".  highest buyer conversion
2. "Living in [Neighborhood] in [City].  Honest Pros and Cons"
3. "[City] Real Estate Market Update.  [Month] [Year]"
4. "How Much Does It Actually Cost to Buy a Home in [City]? ([Year])"

Rules: primary keyword within first 40 characters; year in parentheses at the end; never vague titles.

**Hook structure (first 30 seconds):**
- Seconds 0-5: One sentence stating exactly what this video delivers. Specific. "By the end of this video you'll know exactly what $800,000 buys in Bend, Oregon right now."
- Seconds 5-15: Credibility signal.  not a bio, a relevant credential. "I closed 22 deals in this neighborhood last year."
- Seconds 15-30: Preview the most counterintuitive thing they'll learn. Create a knowledge gap.

**Length sweet spot by content type:**

| Content type | Optimal length |
|---|---|
| Neighborhood guide | 10-15 min |
| Market update | 6-10 min |
| "Moving to" city guide | 10-18 min |
| Listing tour | 5-10 min |
| Buyer/seller FAQ | 5-8 min |
| Luxury immersive tour | 20-60 min (Enes Yilmazer model) |

**Branding: YouTube allows more than other platforms.**
- Branding watermark (logo bug): bottom-right, appears from 0:30 onward. Set in YouTube Studio > Customization > Branding. Use the Ryan Realty navy mark as a transparent PNG. Treat as passive brand signal, not a high-conversion CTA.
- End screen (final 15-20 seconds): subscribe button + related video + logo/wordmark. The end screen is where Ryan Realty branding belongs.  the content earns the right to show the brand.
- Logo in the video frame during the video: **no persistent bug**. End card only.

**Chapters are mandatory** for neighborhood guides and market updates.  YouTube surfaces individual chapters in Google search as "Key Moments."

**Playlist strategy:** Under-used SEO tool. Build playlists: "Moving to Bend, Oregon," "Bend Neighborhood Tours," "Bend Real Estate Market Updates," "Buyer Guide: Bend, Oregon." Optimize each playlist with keyword-rich title and 3-5 sentence description.

**Bend YouTube opportunity:** The niche is under-served. Active channels (Living in Bend Oregon, Lifestyle In Bend Oregon) all sub-50K subscribers. A consistent weekly publishing cadence for 12 months would establish dominance. High-value low-competition queries: "Moving to Bend Oregon 2026," "Living in Tumalo Oregon," "Cost of living Bend Oregon," "Sisters vs Bend Oregon," "Best neighborhoods in Bend Oregon," "Tumalo real estate."

### Facebook

Real estate is 35% of all Meta Lead Ads.  the highest of any industry category. Facebook is the strongest paid lead-gen platform in residential real estate AND reaches the Boomer/Gen X buyer (45-65+) active in Bend's move-up and retirement segment.

**Organic Reels strategy (different from IG Reels):** Facebook skews older. October 2025 algorithm update: Reels uploaded same-day receive 50% more distribution than archived/reposted content. **15-30s is the sweet spot**.  completion rate drops sharply at 45s for this audience. **FB Reels tolerate more overt branding**.  a lower-third with agent name + brokerage does NOT penalize reach the way it does on TikTok or IG. The 45-65 demographic trusts visible branding more than younger short-form audiences.

**Facebook Feed post pattern:**
- 4:5 vertical image/video outperforms 1:1 (takes more mobile screen real estate)
- Links in first comment, never in post body (FB suppresses link posts)
- Caption length: 50-150 characters for peak organic reach.  say the insight, stop
- Multi-image carousel posts generate high swipe engagement and dwell time

**Group strategy.  highest-ROI organic Facebook play:**
Create "Moving to Bend, Oregon" or "Bend Area Homeowners Network".  value-first community, not a listings dump. Apply the Laura Griffin model (7-figure GCI from FB Groups alone): 90% local value content, 10% real estate. Start public to reach 500 members, then switch to private. Cost: $0-$300/month. Expect 60-120 day ramp before steady lead flow.

**Facebook Live:** Algorithm rewards real-time engagement. Best uses: weekly market Q+A, open house walk-throughs, just-listed reveals. Announce 48 hours ahead. 15-30 min engagement sweet spot. Post replay immediately as a normal Reel. Name lower-third is standard and expected in FB Live.  no reach penalty.

**Paid lead-gen.  the 3-act ad creative formula:**

**Act 1.  Pattern Interrupt (first 3 seconds / first line):**
- Specific local stat: "Bend home prices up 8.3% YoY.  but inventory just spiked."
- Local data: "3 homes in NW Crossing closed above list price last week."
- Avoid anything that could be said about any market anywhere.

**Act 2.  Value proposition:**
- Offer specific to Central Oregon: "Bend Market Report.  Q2 2026" outperforms "Free Real Estate Guide" by a wide margin.
- Frame for the buyer: "See what your Bend home is worth today" not "We provide free home valuations."

**Act 3.  CTA:**
- "Get my report" / "See my estimate"
- "Learn More" and "Schedule Now" outperform "Contact Us" in real estate
- "Higher Intent" toggle in Meta Lead Ad settings: adds a confirmation step, reduces volume 40%, but increases qualified lead quality 2-3x. For a principal broker, quality > volume.

**Lead form: 3-5 fields maximum.** Each field above 5 drops completion 5-15%. First contact within 5 minutes converts at 21x the rate of 30-minute contact.

**Cost benchmarks (US real estate, 2026):**
- Average CPL: $16.61
- Cross-industry CPL for context: $27.66
- Max CPL threshold (at $10K commission, 5% close rate): ~$75
- Budget floor for meaningful signal: $300-600/month

**Video vs. static:** Video outperforms static 2.2x for short-form under 30s. Authentic phone-shot selfie-style ad outperforms a $5,000 studio shoot in 9 out of 10 accounts (LeadSync 2026 data).

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn is where the **Bend relocation buyer** actually lives professionally. The Bay Area engineer, Seattle tech lead, and Portland executive making a lifestyle move to Central Oregon is on LinkedIn daily. Bend ranks as the #1 remote work destination nationally (12.1% remote worker growth).

**Top-performing formats 2026:**
- Document/PDF carousel: 6.6-7.0% engagement rate.  3-4x longer dwell time than text; highest algorithmic reward
- Native video: 5.6% engagement rate, +36% YoY; under 30s achieves 200% higher completion
- Text-only: ~2-2.8%; works when the insight is genuinely strong
- External links: ~1.2% (suppressed); 60% reach reduction.  always put links in first comment

**Hook.  first line is everything.** LinkedIn shows only ~210 characters before "See more." Proven hook structures:
- Contrarian data point: "Everyone says the Bend market is cooling. The last 30 days say otherwise."
- Specific number: "We closed 14 listings in Q1 2026. Here's what surprised us."
- Confession/lesson: "I priced a listing wrong last year. Here's what I'd do differently."
- Relocating buyer address: "You're a tech lead in Seattle thinking about Bend. Here are the three questions you actually need answered."

**Length:** 150-250 words for personal posts (up to 3,000 char limit). Paragraphs of 1-3 lines max with white space. "See more" click is a positive algorithm signal.

**Posting cadence:** 2-3x/week, Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM Pacific. Never post daily.  quality dilutes. Plan on 6-9 months before meaningful referral flow; compounding begins around month 9.

**Authority positioning for Matt:**
- LinkedIn headline: "Principal Broker | Bend, Oregon Real Estate | Serving Relocating Buyers + Sellers Since [Year]"
- About section: lead with a data point ("$X in closed volume, Y transactions in Central Oregon"); credibility before personality
- Post framing: "Here's what I'm seeing at the deal table this week".  practitioner with current intelligence

**Content that converts the tech-worker relocation buyer:** School districts, fiber internet coverage in Tumalo/NW Crossing, honest market data charts (they're analytically literate), NW Crossing vs. River West vs. Tetherow differentiation, wildfire insurance costs, Deschutes water rights. What catches out-of-state buyers off guard.  that's the most valuable content on LinkedIn for this audience.

**Logo:** Minimal in feed posts. Logo belongs in profile header/banner, article cover images, document carousel title slide. In feed posts: Matt's name, not the logo. Brand is earned by content quality.

## Captions: the locked spec

This spec applies to all short-form video (IG Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Facebook Reels). No exceptions.

**Font and style:** Geist 500 (medium weight), white text, subtle drop-shadow. NOT on colored pills for general captions. Navy pill with warm stone 2px top border is the spec for news-clip captions (see CLAUDE.md video spec).

**Format.  mandatory (Matt directive 2026-05-07):** Full-sentence, active-word highlight. The entire current sentence stays on screen. The active word is highlighted (navy/gold color accent or scale 1.0→1.08 spring) synced to ElevenLabs forced-alignment timestamps. NEVER word-by-word fade-in/out. NEVER 3-word phrase windows.

**Transitions:** Smooth 200-300ms crossfade between sentences. Previous sentence fades out, next fades in, both partially visible during the window. NEVER hard cut between sentences.

**Timing:** Synced to natural speech cadence via ElevenLabs `/v1/forced-alignment` word-level timestamps.  never to clock-time slots or Remotion Sequence boundaries.

**Placement safe zone (portrait 1080×1920):** y 1480-1720, x 90-990. Captions NEVER render over stats, charts, logos, title cards, animated overlays, or any other visual component. If a competing element occupies the caption zone for a beat, the caption is suppressed for that beat.

**Muted viewers:** 64%-85% of short-form viewers watch muted (TikTok 64%, IG 85%+). Captions carry the video for this audience. Treat them as mandatory, not optional.

## Listing data presentation rules (cross-platform)

The order of information determines whether the viewer stays or leaves.

1. **Price in the hook.  when the price IS the story.** "What $875,000 gets you in NW Crossing" works as a hook because it creates an immediate expectation gap. Lead with price when the price is surprising relative to what's shown.
2. **Lifestyle first, specs last.** "3 beds, 2 baths, 1,800 sqft" is the last thing said, never the first. The bedroom count is filter information.  it belongs in the caption or swipe card.
3. **City/neighborhood name always early.** Geographic discovery (Instagram search, TikTok OCR, YouTube keyword matching) depends on the place name appearing in the first few seconds.
4. **Units always present.** "$895,000" not "895K." "4 bedrooms" not "4 BR." Per CLAUDE.md video hard rules.
5. **One specific detail that earns a click.** For listing posts: the one feature that's genuinely notable.  the view, the Deschutes access, the garage workshop. Not a list of every feature.
