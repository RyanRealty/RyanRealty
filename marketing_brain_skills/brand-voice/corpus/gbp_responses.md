## Pattern summary (for the brain)

**Average response length.** ~75 words for short reviews, ~140 words for longer reviews.

**Opening patterns (frequency).**
- "Thank you so much for..." (10/23)
- "[Name], thank you for..." (8/23)
- "Thank you for..." (3/23)
- "This genuinely made my day..." (1/23, but flagged as a high-trust signature)

**Closing patterns.**
- Forward-looking well-wish ("Wishing you joy in your new home," "Wishing you all the best in your new chapter") (8/23)
- Open-door offer ("I'm always here if you need anything down the road," "please don't hesitate to reach out") (5/23)
- Plain gratitude close ("Thank you again," "Best regards, Matt") (10/23)

**Recurring lexical signature.**
genuinely, honored, privilege, trust, chapter, finish line, the unpredictable market, small business, mean the world, makes all the difference

**Recurring emotional move.**
1. Open with gratitude.
2. Name a specific reason the client was great to work with (not generic).
3. Position Ryan Realty as small and grateful (not corporate).
4. Close with forward-looking warmth and an open door.

**Sentence length range.** 8 to 35 words. Median ~18 words. Long enough to be warm, short enough to never feel padded.

**First-person mix.** "I" when responding to a one-on-one transaction Matt personally handled. "We" when crediting the team (e.g., Rebecca on Lauren Dewey's review). Both feel natural.

**Punctuation.** Frequent commas. Occasional ellipses-like commas where a normal speaker would pause. Rare exclamation marks, never more than one per response. No semicolons in his typing.
