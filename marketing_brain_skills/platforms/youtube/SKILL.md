## 1. Platform overview

YouTube is a long-duration, high-intent channel.  not a scroll feed. Viewers arrive because they searched for something or a recommendation matched a prior watch pattern. That distinction changes everything about how Ryan Realty should use it.

For a real estate brokerage in a mid-size market like Bend, YouTube serves three distinct jobs that no other channel does as well:

**Discovery at the top of the funnel.** Buyers relocating from out of state (a historically large Bend segment) search "living in Bend Oregon," "Bend Oregon neighborhood guide," "Bend Oregon real estate market 2026" months before they contact an agent. A useful video that ranks for those queries is a persistent asset.  unlike a paid Meta ad, it keeps working with zero incremental spend.

**Trust-building before first contact.** YouTube is where strangers decide whether a broker sounds knowledgeable or is just running ads. A 12-minute market report that cites real data earns more trust than a dozen Reels. The format signals depth.

**SEO spillover onto Google.** YouTube is owned by Google. Video results appear in Google web search, often in the first three positions for real estate + local queries. A well-optimized video ranks in two search engines simultaneously.

Where YouTube fits in the Ryan Realty content strategy: at the top and middle of the funnel. Short-form (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) drives awareness. YouTube drives education and conviction. The email list and FUB convert. A viewer who watches 8+ minutes of a Ryan Realty market report has self-qualified as a serious buyer or seller.  they are warm by the time they submit a contact form.

Secondary use: a permanent library. Every market video produced for short-form can be extended into a long-form YouTube cut. The short-form version drives initial distribution; the long-form version ranks in search over time.

## 2. Algorithm primer

YouTube's recommendation system operates across three distinct surfaces, each with different ranking signals. Understanding the surfaces is more useful than talking about "the algorithm" as a monolith.

### The three surfaces

**Search**.  User-initiated. Viewer types a query, YouTube returns results. Ranking factors here are closest to traditional SEO: title/description relevance to the query, plus performance signals (CTR on that query, watch time for that query, engagement generated). A video optimized for "Bend Oregon real estate market" will appear for that specific intent. Search is the most durable surface.  a video that ranks for a query today may still rank in 18 months with no further promotion.

**Suggested (Up Next)**.  Appears alongside and after the video currently playing. This is the highest-volume surface on YouTube.  it accounts for the majority of views on most channels. The algorithm matches on topic relevance to the video just watched, plus channel affinity and viewer watch history. A real estate buyer watching one Ryan Realty market report will see the next one suggested immediately. This is the compounding surface.  each video you publish that performs well creates demand for the others.

**Browse (Home feed)**.  Personalized to the viewer's total watch history. The algorithm uses collaborative filtering: viewers who watch the same content you do are shown your videos. New uploads get a brief window of Browse distribution to existing subscribers.  if those subscribers click and watch, the video gets pushed to the broader lookalike audience. If they ignore it, Browse distribution collapses quickly.

### What signals matter (in rank order by impact, per YouTube's own research and Backlinko's 2024 correlation study)

1. **Average view duration (absolute watch time)**.  How many minutes does an average viewer watch? For long-form content, this is the most powerful signal. A 12-minute video where viewers average 9 minutes ranks significantly better than a 12-minute video averaging 3 minutes. (Source: Backlinko YouTube Ranking Factors study; YouTube Creator Academy.)

2. **Average view percentage (audience retention rate)**.  What percentage of the video does the average viewer watch? Directionally the same signal as #1, but normalized for length. This is the metric the ingestor marks as the "primary algorithm signal" for the video scope. For short videos, this dominates. YouTube's Creator Academy states that high relative retention is a stronger signal for short content; absolute watch time is stronger for long content.

3. **CTR (impressions click-through rate)**.  Of every viewer who saw the thumbnail, what percentage clicked? This is a real-time feedback loop. A video with a high CTR gets more distribution; more distribution generates more data; that data either confirms or kills the broader push. YouTube itself notes that most channels see CTR between 2-10%, with the top quartile of small channels hitting 5-8% for well-optimized thumbnails. (Source: YouTube Help Center, confirmed by vidIQ 2024 reporting.) Niche channels with a defined audience can see higher CTR because their subscriber base is highly pre-qualified.

4. **Session time (session initiation and continuation)**.  How often does a viewer's YouTube session start from your video, and how much total watch time does your video generate for the platform, including downstream videos they watch after yours? Videos that drive session starts (a viewer opens YouTube, clicks your video first) receive preferential Browse distribution. YouTube's algorithm is partly optimized for total platform time, not just your video's time.

5. **Engagement signals (likes, comments, shares, saves)**.  Backlinko's 2024 study found comments have the strongest first-page correlation of the engagement signals. Subscriptions generated from a video (subscriptions_gained) are also a strong signal.  YouTube interprets "this video made someone subscribe" as a quality marker. Likes matter but are harder for viewers to generate organically. Shares drive session starts.

6. **Freshness + upload recency**.  Only matters in the first 24-72 hours for the Browse surface (Subscribers feed). After that, recency matters far less than performance. There is no "upload frequency" signal that helps dormant videos.  posting often only helps if each video performs.

### What does NOT affect algorithm distribution
- Tags (confirmed weak correlation per Backlinko; YouTube uses them only for spelling correction)
- Exact-match keywords in titles (semantic understanding now dominates)
- Keyword-optimized descriptions (zero correlation with rankings per Backlinko, though descriptions help Suggested video matching)
- Upload consistency or frequency as a standalone signal (YouTube has confirmed this explicitly)
- Channel monetization status

## 3. Metrics deep-dive

All metrics below are tracked by the ingestor. Format: what it measures / good for a 10K-sub niche channel / good for real estate specifically / causes of underperformance / levers.

---

### Account scope

**views**
What it measures: total video view count for the channel on a given day, across all videos.
Good (10K-sub niche channel): 200-800 views/day is healthy organic; spikes 2-5x above baseline when a new video launches.
Good (real estate): 100-400 views/day for a local market channel; higher in spring buying season (March-June) and lower in August-September historically.
Underperformance causes: no new content in 30+ days, low CTR on recent uploads, mismatched audience (subscribers from old content that no longer matches current output).
Levers: new uploads, thumbnail refresh on evergreen content, internal end-screen links between videos.

**watch_time_minutes**
What it measures: total minutes of video watched across all channel content in the day.
Good (10K-sub niche): 1,500-5,000 minutes/day. Watch time is the primary monetization and ranking currency.
Good (real estate): long-form market reports (10-15 min) compress a lot of watch time per view. One video watched fully is worth more than five Shorts watched 40%.
Underperformance causes: short average video length, high drop-off in first 30 seconds, unengaged subscriber base.
Levers: produce longer content (market reports, neighborhood tours), improve first-30-second hook to reduce early abandonment, series format (each episode ends with a teaser for the next).

**subscribers_gained**
What it measures: new subscribers acquired in the day.
Good (10K-sub niche): 5-30/day is healthy organic growth. Days with a new upload typically spike 3-10x above baseline.
Good (real estate): 3-15/day. Real estate has a narrow addressable audience (people actively buying/selling in a specific geography), so absolute numbers are lower than broad-interest channels.
Underperformance causes: low video quality, content not targeting the right search intent, no clear value proposition stated early in videos ("why subscribe").
Levers: explicit CTA to subscribe at the 60-second mark (after the viewer has self-selected as interested), end-screen subscription module, series content (viewer subscribes to not miss the next episode).

**subscribers_lost**
What it measures: subscribers who unsubscribed in the day.
Good (10K-sub niche): churn below 30% of daily gained is healthy. Net positive is the target.
Good (real estate): some churn is natural after a buyer closes.  they've gotten what they needed. A sudden spike (2x+ baseline) suggests content-audience mismatch (e.g., posting off-topic content to a real-estate-subscribed audience).
Underperformance causes: content pivot, posting irrelevant content, posting frequency that overwhelms subscribers who subscribed for monthly content.
Levers: consistent content category, clear channel positioning in the channel description and banner.

**average_view_duration_seconds**
What it measures: mean seconds watched per view across all videos on that day.
Good (10K-sub niche): 180-360 seconds (3-6 minutes). Higher for long-form content if audience is engaged.
Good (real estate): property tour content typically holds 40-55% of the video length; market reports from trusted sources can hold 60-70% for engaged buyers. (Operator estimate.  no public real-estate-specific benchmark exists.)
Underperformance causes: weak hook (dropping viewers in first 30s), content that doesn't deliver the promise of the thumbnail/title, slow pacing.
Levers: tighter editing (cut 20% of runtime from every video as a baseline), front-load the payoff ("here's what I found" before "here's how I found it"), use B-roll to maintain visual interest during data explanations.

**average_view_percentage**
What it measures: average fraction (%) of video watched per view, across all videos for that day.
Good (10K-sub niche): 40-60% is considered solid for YouTube. Above 60% is excellent. Below 30% indicates a retention problem.
Good (real estate): property tour content tends to drop below the industry average (buyers skip to rooms they care about). Educational/market content from a trusted source can sustain 50-65% if the script is tight.
Algorithm relevance: YouTube's own materials (Creator Academy, 2024 Help Center) identify this as a key factor in search and suggested distribution.  the single most important per-video retention metric the ingestor captures.
Underperformance causes: misleading thumbnail/title (bait-and-switch effect), slow intro, padding, irrelevant B-roll.
Levers: pattern interrupts at 25%, 50%, and 75% marks to re-hook exiting viewers; chapter markers (so viewers skip to what they want and stay longer than if they bounce entirely); stronger scripting discipline (no filler, no repetition).

**card_click_rate**
What it measures: percentage of viewers who clicked a card overlay (a linked video, playlist, or external link shown during playback).
Good: 0.5-2% is typical. Real estate content rarely exceeds 1% without a deliberate verbal CTA pointing to the card.
Underperformance causes: cards placed at wrong timestamp (placing at minute 10 of a video where most viewers have already left), no verbal CTA.
Levers: place cards at 40-60% through the video (when retention is still reasonable), combine with a verbal "tap the card in the top right" CTA. Use to link related videos in the same series.

**card_impressions**
What it measures: total number of times cards were shown to viewers.
Use: directional.  tracks whether cards are being displayed at all. If card_impressions is near zero, cards are not configured. If card_click_rate is low relative to impressions, the card placement or CTA is the issue, not card setup.

**annotation_click_through_rate**
What it measures: CTR on legacy annotation overlays (pre-2019 feature, now largely replaced by cards and end screens). This will return 0 for all new content. Included for completeness in backfill data.

**likes**
What it measures: likes received in the day across all channel content.
Good: a like-to-view ratio of 2-5% is healthy for a niche channel. Real estate content tends toward the lower end (viewers who found useful information may not think to like).
Levers: verbal like CTA mid-video ("if this breakdown was useful, hit like.  it helps me know what to cover next"). The ask must be natural and specific.

**comments**
What it measures: comments received across all content.
Algorithm importance: Backlinko's 2024 ranking study found comments have the highest correlation with first-page YouTube Search rankings of any engagement metric. A commented-on video is interpreted as a discussion-worthy video.
Good: 0.5-2% of views results in a comment for niche channels. Even 5-10 comments per 500 views is healthy for a local real estate channel.
Levers: ask specific answerable questions ("what neighborhood in Bend are you most interested in.  drop it below"); respond to every comment in the first 48 hours (this generates a reply notification that brings commenters back and inflates total comment count).

**shares**
What it measures: times viewers shared a video to another platform or via link.
Algorithm importance: shares drive session starts.  when a shared video is the entry point to a YouTube session, the algorithm credits the video with initiating high-value engagement. Backlinko found shares have strong first-page correlation.
Levers: create content with a clear "send to someone" hook ("forward this to anyone thinking about moving to Bend in the next 6 months"); local market reports are naturally share-worthy between relocation contacts.

### Video scope (per-video metrics, top-15 by views)

**views (video)**.  Same definition but scoped to the individual video. Track this per video to identify which titles/thumbnails/topics drive discovery.

**watch_time_minutes (video)**.  The primary input into the algorithm for long-form. A single video generating 10,000 watch-minutes/day is worth more algorithmically than 10 videos each generating 1,000.

**average_view_duration_seconds (video)**.  Per-video hook and pacing quality indicator. Compare against video total length to derive the implicit retention rate when average_view_percentage is not available.

**average_view_percentage (video)**.  The most important per-video metric the ingestor captures. This is the retention score the algorithm uses for Suggested and Browse distribution. If this drops on a video below 30%, the algorithm will reduce distribution on that video regardless of view count.

**subscribers_gained (video)**.  Which videos convert viewers into subscribers. A video that drives high subscribers_gained is a format/topic signal: make more like it.

**impressions (video)**.  Total thumbnail impressions served for this video on any YouTube surface. Required to compute true CTR: impressions_click_through_rate = clicks / impressions. If impressions are low, the algorithm has not tested the video widely.  it's a distribution problem, not a click-quality problem.

**impressions_click_through_rate (video)**.  The thumbnail and title quality score. YouTube shows the thumbnail to a sample audience; CTR determines whether distribution expands. Industry benchmark: 2-10% across all content; 4-7% is competitive for a niche channel. Below 2% suggests thumbnail/title mismatch with audience expectation. Above 8% on a niche topic is strong.

## 4. Format playbook

### Long-form (10-20 minutes).  the primary Ryan Realty YouTube format

Best for: monthly market reports, neighborhood deep-dives, buyer/seller education series, relocation guides for specific cities (Bend, Sisters, Redmond, Sunriver).

Why it wins on YouTube: longer videos generate more watch time per view, rank better in Search for high-intent queries, and allow for the kind of nuanced market explanation that builds genuine authority. A buyer comparing Bend to other markets does not want a 90-second Reel.  they want the full picture from someone who knows.

Title pattern: front-load the most specific, searchable phrase. "Bend Oregon Real Estate Market.  May 2026 Data" outperforms "May Market Update.  What's Happening in Bend." The former matches the search query; the latter requires the viewer to already know they want Ryan Realty.

Thumbnail pattern: one dominant number or claim, one face (real, not stock), no more than 5 words of text. Real estate thumbnails that show a specific dollar figure (e.g., "$498K Median") outperform generic "Market Update" titles. The number creates intrigue even before the viewer reads the title.

CTA pattern: three-point structure.  (1) watch hook at 0:00-0:30 ("Here's what the data shows for May"); (2) midpoint engagement ask at ~50% ("If you're seeing Bend for the first time, subscribe.  I post this every month"); (3) end-screen at final 20 seconds linking to the most recent prior episode.

First-15-second pattern: state the most surprising or specific finding immediately. "In May, median prices in Bend dropped for the third month in a row.  but inventory is still tight enough that sellers are fine." This gives a searching viewer immediate confirmation they're in the right video.

### Shorts (under 60 seconds).  supporting format, not primary

Best for: repurposing a single stat or insight from a long-form video, driving Browse discovery to the channel, A/B testing thumbnail concepts with a lower-effort format.

YouTube Shorts has a separate algorithm from long-form. Retention on Shorts is measured differently (loop rate, not average percentage). Shorts can drive subscribers but those subscribers tend to churn faster if they discover the channel primarily publishes long-form.

Strategy for Ryan Realty: use Shorts to surface one strong data point from each monthly market report. Link to the full video via the Shorts description. Do not treat Shorts as a replacement for long-form.  they are a top-of-funnel sampler.

### Livestreams.  situational use

Best for: Q&A after a market report, real-time coverage of a significant market event (rate decision, major policy change), interactive sessions for active buyer/seller leads.

Livestreams generate strong engagement signals (comments, real-time reactions) and receive preferential Browse placement during the stream. The recorded version retains some of those engagement signals in the algorithm. However, they require scheduling, promotion, and real-time availability.  the investment is only worth it for genuinely timely content or for a channel that has already built a live audience (typically 5K+ active subscribers).

Not recommended as a primary format for Ryan Realty at the current stage.

## 5. Posting cadence and timing

### Frequency

Research consensus (confirmed by YouTube's own Creator Academy guidance and Social Media Examiner analysis): upload frequency itself is not a ranking signal. What matters is that each video performs when published. One high-quality video per month that generates strong average view percentage and watch time does more for algorithmic distribution than four low-quality videos.

For a real estate channel at Ryan Realty's stage (local market, sub-10K subscribers), the sustainable cadence is:

- **Monthly market report** (long-form, 10-15 min).  the anchor. Timed to data availability.
- **1-2 supplementary videos per month**.  neighborhood guides, buyer education, listing tours extended into YouTube format. These diversify search-surface coverage without requiring a new research cycle.
- **Shorts** as derivative content.  no additional production, pulled from existing long-form.

Going below one upload per month risks losing the Browse distribution window for new subscribers and suppresses channel-level signals. Going above 4 per month without corresponding quality creates diminishing returns and can cannibalize each video's algorithm test window.

### Timing

YouTube's own guidance (Help Center, Creator Academy) explicitly states that upload timing and day-of-week are not ranking signals.  the algorithm does not give preferential treatment to videos uploaded at a specific time.

However, timing does affect the initial subscriber notification window. Subscribers who have recently engaged with the channel are most likely to see a new video notification within 24 hours of upload. The best window for real estate content (weekend home-search behavior, after-work browsing): **Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm Pacific** tends to generate the fastest initial subscriber engagement in western US markets. This maximizes the "first 48 hours" Browse test window.

Avoid Friday uploads (viewer behavior shifts to entertainment content on weekends, not research). Avoid publishing the same week as a major national news event that dominates attention.

## 6. SEO on YouTube

### Keyword research approach

1. Use YouTube's own search bar autocomplete as the primary keyword signal. Type "Bend Oregon real estate" and record all autocomplete suggestions.  these are high-volume, real queries from YouTube's data. No tool needed.

2. Complement with Google autocomplete for the same terms.  many YouTube searches originate from Google video results, so Google's autocomplete reveals cross-platform intent.

3. Review the "Related searches" at the bottom of YouTube results pages for target queries. These surface secondary and long-tail terms.

4. Tools like TubeBuddy and vidIQ layer search volume estimates and competition scores on top of YouTube autocomplete data. Useful for competitive research but not required for a local market channel where the keyword space is naturally narrow.

### Titles

- Lead with the most searchable phrase. Example: "Bend Oregon Real Estate Market Report | May 2026".  the pipe separator is a common format that signals both the topic and the recency.
- Keep under 60 characters to avoid truncation on mobile.
- Do not keyword-stuff.  YouTube's semantic understanding means exact match is less important than relevance and clarity.
- Front-load specificity: month, market, key finding.

### Descriptions

- First 150 characters appear in search results before truncation.  this is the description snippet. Lead with the clearest one-sentence statement of what the video covers.
- Include natural-language variants of your target keywords in the first paragraph (not stuffed, just written normally).
- Chapter timestamps in the description (00:00 Introduction / 02:30 Active inventory / 06:00 Median price / 10:00 Buyer takeaways) improve average view percentage by allowing viewers to navigate directly to relevant sections instead of bouncing. Chapters also create chapter-level search indexing on Google.
- Include a link to the Ryan Realty website and relevant landing pages in the description.

### Tags

Per Backlinko's 2024 study and YouTube's own confirmation, tags have minimal ranking impact. Use them for spelling variants and abbreviations (e.g., "OR real estate", "Central Oregon housing market") but do not spend significant time on tag optimization.

### End screens

End screens appear in the final 20 seconds. Add two elements: (1) the most recent upload or a recommended related video and (2) a subscribe button. These directly drive watch_time_minutes through session continuation and subscribers_gained.

### Cards

Place cards at the 40-60% mark of a video, paired with a verbal CTA. Link to the most relevant related video. Cards placed in the last 10% of a video are largely wasted.  most viewers who make it to the end will see the end screen anyway.

## 8. Improvement tactics

Ordered by impact-to-effort ratio for a channel at Ryan Realty's current stage.

**1. Optimize thumbnail CTR on the 5 highest-impression videos.**
Metric: impressions_click_through_rate. Expected lift: +1-3 percentage points on CTR translates to +20-60% more views from the same impressions pool. Lead time: 48-72 hours to see algorithm response after re-upload. (Source: vidIQ CTR improvement case studies, 2024.) Method: A/B test thumbnails via YouTube's built-in A/B test feature (requires 1K+ subs). One number, one face, high contrast.

**2. Add chapter timestamps to every existing video without them.**
Metric: average_view_percentage. Expected lift: +5-15 percentage points in average retention for content-heavy videos (viewers who would otherwise bounce now skip to their relevant section and stay). Lead time: algorithm re-evaluation typically within 72 hours of metadata update. (Source: YouTube Creator Academy chapter documentation.) Method: add timestamps to the description.  no re-upload required.

**3. Script and film a 12-minute monthly market report for each of the next 3 months.**
Metric: watch_time_minutes (account), average_view_duration_seconds. Expected lift: each long-form video with strong retention generates 5-15x the watch time per view of a Short. Three months of consistent uploads builds the "channel activity" signal. Lead time: cumulative.  starts compounding after 60-90 days. (Source: YouTube's research on how watch time accumulates across a catalog.)

**4. Create a "playlist" for each content type (Market Reports, Neighborhood Guides, Buyer Tips).**
Metric: views, watch_time_minutes. Expected lift: playlists drive session continuation.  YouTube auto-plays the next video in a playlist, generating additional views and watch time without requiring re-engagement. (Source: YouTube Help Center, Playlists section.) Lead time: immediate on creation; compounding over 30 days.

**5. Respond to every comment within 48 hours.**
Metric: comments (channel-level), subscribers_gained. Expected lift: comment responses generate reply notifications that return the commenter, doubling the effective comment count and signaling an engaged creator to the algorithm. Estimated +10-20% comment thread depth. (Operator estimate; aligns with Backlinko's finding that comment count correlates with first-page rankings.) Lead time: immediate.

**6. Add a verbal subscribe CTA at the 60-second mark of every new video.**
Metric: subscribers_gained. Expected lift: explicit CTAs increase subscriber conversion rate by 20-40% for viewers who have self-selected past the first minute. (Source: Social Media Examiner, YouTube growth tactics, 2024.) Must be authentic and specific ("Subscribe if you want the June data when it drops").  not a generic "like and subscribe."

**7. Publish one "evergreen" neighborhood guide per quarter.**
Metric: views (long-term, compounding). Expected lift: evergreen search-intent content (neighborhood cost of living, schools, commute times) accumulates views over 12-24 months. A single well-optimized guide can generate 500-2,000 views/month indefinitely. (Operator estimate from pattern analysis of similar local market channels.) Lead time: 3-6 months before Search rankings mature.

**8. Use end screens consistently with a "next video" recommendation.**
Metric: watch_time_minutes (session continuation). Expected lift: well-configured end screens increase session continuation by 10-25% for viewers who complete the video. (Source: YouTube Creator Academy end-screen guidance.) The linked video should be thematically adjacent to what was just watched, not the channel's most recent upload.

**9. Publish market report within 5 business days of data availability each month.**
Metric: views (freshness spike), subscribers_gained. Expected lift: recency matters in the first 48-72 hours for Browse distribution. Publishing market data the same week it's available captures the "news" behavior of viewers searching for current figures. Delayed publishing loses the news-search window. (Operator estimate.)

**10. Test Shorts repurposed from the most shareable 60 seconds of each long-form video.**
Metric: views (channel-wide), subscribers_gained. Expected lift: Shorts that surface in YouTube's Short feed drive channel subscribers who then watch long-form. Estimated 5-15 new subscribers per 10K Short views for a well-matched audience. Lead time: 1-4 weeks for Shorts distribution to peak. (Operator estimate; aligns with Ryan Pineda's publicly documented strategy.)

## 9. Anti-patterns

**Treating posting frequency as a growth lever.** YouTube's algorithm does not reward frequency.  it rewards performance. Posting two mediocre videos per week will underperform one strong video per month, and the mediocre videos will drag down channel-level signals. Never post to fill a calendar.

**Optimizing tags heavily.** Tags have minimal ranking impact (Backlinko, 2024). Time spent on tag research is better spent on thumbnail design or title optimization, which have proven impact.

**Keyword-stuffed descriptions.** Descriptions with dense keyword repetition look like spam to viewers and provide negligible ranking benefit. Write naturally.

**Publishing long videos that don't justify the length.** A 15-minute video that should be 8 minutes is a retention killer. Every minute of padding drops average view percentage. Edit ruthlessly. The algorithm sees the drop.

**Ignoring retention curves.** YouTube Studio's retention graph shows exactly where viewers leave. Ignoring this data is ignoring the clearest signal the algorithm provides about what to fix. Review the retention curve for every video within 7 days of upload and identify the drop-off points.

**Starting every video with "hey guys, welcome back."** The first 5 seconds determine whether a viewer stays. Any generic opener wastes those seconds. State the most compelling finding immediately. This is the same rule as the CLAUDE.md video hook requirements.  it applies to long-form YouTube just as strictly.

**Using AI-generated thumbnails or stock photography as the hero image.** YouTube viewers have trained themselves to recognize fake or generic real estate visuals. A real photo of a real Bend location with a real number performs better than a polished composite. Authenticity is a competitive advantage in local real estate content.

**Conflating YouTube Shorts subscribers with qualified buyers.** Shorts attract broad audiences, many of whom are not in the real estate market. A subscriber gained from a Shorts clip about "10 things to know before moving to Bend" is worth less than a subscriber gained from a market report, because the former is curious and the latter is active. Do not over-optimize for subscriber count.  optimize for subscriber quality.

**Publishing brand-centric content.** The channel should be about the market and the community, not about Ryan Realty. Brand-first content.  logo at the top, agent intro in the first 30 seconds, brokerage tagline throughout.  drives the skip rate up. The brand earns trust by providing genuinely useful information, not by announcing itself. This aligns directly with the brand-first-not-broker-first directive in CLAUDE.md memory.

**Chasing trending topics outside real estate.** YouTube's algorithm builds a viewer profile. A real estate channel that publishes a finance trend video or a lifestyle video to chase views confuses the algorithm about who the channel is for, dilutes the subscriber base, and reduces the channel's topical authority signal. Every video should be something a serious Bend buyer or seller would find valuable.

## Brain-readable summary

### If metric X drops, do Y

- **If average_view_percentage drops below 35% on a new video:** pull the retention graph within 72 hours. Identify the first significant drop-off timestamp. If it's in the first 60 seconds, the hook is the problem (rewrite the opening for the next video). If it's at a mid-point, the pacing collapsed there.  cut or restructure that section in future content.

- **If impressions_click_through_rate drops below 2% on a high-impression video:** the thumbnail or title is mismatched with the audience. Redesign the thumbnail (one number, one face, high contrast) and update the title to front-load the most specific searchable phrase. Expect 48-72 hours for the algorithm to re-evaluate.

- **If subscribers_gained drops below 5/day for two consecutive weeks:** the most recent content is not converting discovery views into subscribers. Review which videos are generating views but not subscribers (available in YouTube Studio's traffic source report). The CTA is likely absent or weak.  add a midpoint verbal subscribe ask to the next three videos.

### The 3 levers most likely to lift watch_time_minutes

1. **Publish longer content that earns its length.** A single 12-minute market report with 55% retention generates more watch time than six 90-second Shorts watched to completion. Long-form is the watch-time lever, not Shorts.

2. **Improve the first-30-second hook to cut early abandonment.** If 40% of viewers leave in the first 30 seconds, fixing that alone increases average watch time by 20-40% without changing anything else about the video.

3. **Use playlists and end-screen continuation to chain views.** Session continuation means every video that finishes playing auto-starts the next. Watch time from session continuation is algorithmically equivalent to direct watch time.

### The single most important metric for the YouTube algorithm in 2026

**Average view percentage (audience retention rate)**.  specifically at the per-video level.

This is the metric that determines whether a video receives expanded Suggested and Browse distribution. CTR determines whether viewers click; retention determines whether YouTube keeps promoting the video after the initial test window. A video with a 65% retention rate that generates 500 views in week one will continue to grow. A video with a 20% retention rate that generates 10,000 views in week one will plateau and be suppressed. The ingestor captures this as `average_view_percentage` at both the account scope (channel health) and the video scope (per-video algorithm signal). This is the metric to watch first, every time.
