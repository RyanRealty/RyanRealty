## 1. Executive summary

Ryan Realty enters Q3 2026 as Bend's most data-rigorously positioned independent brokerage. The brand has a documented market voice (voice_guidelines.md v1.5), a three-broker team (Matt Ryan, Paul Stevenson, Rebecca Peterson), an autonomous content pipeline capable of producing listing reels, market report videos, neighborhood guides, blog posts, and social drops with minimal manual intervention, and a Facebook seller growth pipeline generating qualified seller leads week-over-week.

The Q3 2026 strategy has one overriding purpose: build the content loop to the point where it generates a predictable, growing share of qualified seller leads without requiring proportional increases in paid ad spend. Every tactical decision in Q3 flows from this purpose.

The six strategic objective layers for Q3, in priority order:

1. **North star:** Grow qualified seller leads per month from the Q2 baseline to a Q3 target of [baseline TBD from Supabase marketing_channel_daily query].
2. **Brand position:** Establish Ryan Realty as the recognized data-authority brokerage in Bend across at least three measurable signals (branded search volume, share of voice on Bend real estate queries, GBP review velocity).
3. **Channel growth:** Reach 5,000 Instagram followers, 3,000 YouTube subscribers, 2,000 Facebook followers, and 1,000 LinkedIn connections by end of Q3.
4. **Site health:** Achieve top-5 organic ranking for 10 of 30 target seller-intent queries by end of Q3. Improve home-valuation tool completion rate from its Q2 baseline.
5. **Ad health:** Maintain Facebook seller campaign CPL below $65 per qualified lead. Achieve CTR above 1.8% on the primary seller-intent creative.
6. **Operational hygiene:** Maintain GBP rating at or above 4.9 stars. Achieve zero FUB tagging drift (all leads tagged within 24 hours). Maintain email deliverability above 97%.

The Q3 content calendar targets 52 short-form social posts (one per week per platform, weighted to Instagram and Facebook), four monthly market report videos, one YouTube long-form per month, two SEO blog posts per month, one email newsletter per month, and one neighborhood guide per month.

The autonomous pipeline (brain layer + 50+ producers + asset library + approval queue) executes this calendar with Matt's approval at each deliverable gate. The pipeline does not publish without Matt's explicit per-asset approval.

## 4. Strategic objectives and the six-layer model

The six-layer model is the hierarchy used by the autonomous pipeline's generate-briefs skill to score and prioritize action rows. The layers are ordered by weight in the priority_score formula.

**The priority score formula:**

```
priority_score = 0.50 × north_star_impact
              + 0.20 × brand_position_lift
              + 0.15 × channel_growth
              + 0.10 × site_or_ad_health
              + 0.05 × brand_equity
```

Each factor scales from 0 to 1. A listing video for a $900,000 NW Crossing home has high north_star_impact (0.8, because it serves a high-price listing that generates a strong commission if sold, and signals Ryan Realty's market range to other NW Crossing homeowners) and moderate brand_position_lift (0.5, because it adds to the content library but does not directly drive the data-authority brand position). A monthly market report video has lower north_star_impact (0.4, indirect path to seller leads) but very high brand_position_lift (0.9) and high channel_growth (0.7, consistently drives YouTube and Instagram follower growth).

The six layers, with Q3 targets:

**Layer 1: North star (50% weight)**
- Definition: Qualified seller leads per month. A qualified seller lead is a Deschutes County homeowner who has expressed intent to sell within 12 months and has not yet listed with another brokerage.
- Q2 baseline: [TBD from Supabase marketing_channel_daily query: channel='fub', metric_name='qualified_seller_leads_mtd', avg of last 3 months].
- Q3 target: 20% increase over Q2 baseline. If Q2 baseline is 8 leads/month, Q3 target is 10 leads/month.
- Primary drivers: Facebook seller campaign, market report content (content-attributed leads), GBP review velocity.

**Layer 2: Brand position (20% weight)**
- Definition: Share of voice for Bend real estate queries. Proxy metrics: branded search volume (Google Search Console), Instagram account mentions, press mentions.
- Q3 targets: Branded search volume (queries for "Ryan Realty Bend" or "Matt Ryan real estate") growing month-over-month. Share of voice proxy: Ryan Realty content appears in top-3 results for 5 of 10 non-branded Bend real estate queries by end of Q3.
- Primary drivers: Monthly market report, SEO blog posts, GBP presence.

**Layer 3: Channel growth (15% weight)**
- Definition: Net follower/subscriber/connection growth on each platform.
- Q3 channel targets (see §10 for per-channel detail): Instagram 5,000 followers, YouTube 3,000 subscribers, Facebook 2,000 followers, LinkedIn 1,000 connections.
- Primary drivers: Consistent posting cadence, Reels, market data content.

**Layer 4: Site health (10% weight)**
- Definition: Organic keyword rankings, Core Web Vitals, home-valuation tool completion rate.
- Q3 targets: Top-5 organic ranking for 10 target queries. Home-valuation completion rate from [TBD baseline] to [TBD target].
- Primary drivers: SEO blog posts, site performance optimizations.

**Layer 5: Ad health (10% weight)**
- Definition: Facebook seller campaign CPL, CTR, creative fatigue score.
- Q3 targets: CPL below $65. CTR above 1.8%. Zero creative fatigue triggers.
- Primary drivers: Weekly audit-ads cycle, quarterly creative refresh.

**Layer 6: Operational hygiene (5% weight)**
- Definition: GBP rating, FUB tag cleanliness, email deliverability.
- Q3 targets: GBP at or above 4.9 stars. FUB lead tagging within 24 hours, zero untagged leads older than 48 hours. Email deliverability above 97%.
- Primary drivers: ops-reputation producer, ops-fub-crm producer, Resend domain verification.

## 5. The north star: qualified seller leads

The north star is qualified seller leads per month. This section explains why this metric was chosen, how it is measured, how it connects to revenue, and how every Q3 tactic ladders to it.

### 5.1 Why qualified seller leads

Real estate brokerage revenue is generated at closing. A closed transaction on the seller side generates a commission of approximately 2.5-3% of the sale price in the current Oregon market. A $675,000 median Bend SFR generates approximately $16,875-$20,250 in gross commission income (GCI) at close on the listing side.

Seller leads are the input to the listing pipeline. Buyer leads are also valuable, but in a single-market firm with three brokers, the highest-leverage use of marketing spend is building the seller pipeline. Sellers bring the inventory. The inventory attracts buyers. The brokerage represents sellers at a known GCI per transaction.

The decision to name seller leads (not buyer leads, not total transaction volume, not total GCI) as the north star reflects the Reforge framework insight (see framework-references.md §1): the content loop that Ryan Realty is building is optimized for seller-intent audiences. The Facebook seller growth pipeline (docs/FACEBOOK_SELLER_GROWTH_PIPELINE.md) is built for sellers. The market report content speaks primarily to sellers (homeowners evaluating whether to sell). Naming seller leads as the north star aligns the entire pipeline.

### 5.2 How it is measured

Qualified seller leads are tracked in FUB (Follow Up Boss) CRM. A lead is classified as "qualified seller" when it meets all three criteria:
1. The contact owns a property in Deschutes County (verified via FUB property field or conversation note).
2. The contact has expressed intent to sell within 12 months (captured via intake form, conversation note, or FUB stage assignment).
3. The contact has not listed with another brokerage as of the measurement date.

The measurement source is `public.marketing_channel_daily` where `channel='fub'` and `metric_name='qualified_seller_leads_mtd'`. The audit-crm skill reads this metric and surfaces it to generate-briefs as the primary north-star signal.

### 5.3 Revenue model

At a $675,000 median price and a 2.75% listing commission:

- Per qualified seller lead that converts to a listing: approximately $18,500 GCI (assuming median price and standard commission).
- Conversion rate: industry average listing-lead-to-close is approximately 15-20% for well-managed pipelines. At 20%, 10 qualified leads per month yields approximately 2 listings per month at a 5-month average sales cycle (initial contact to close). [Source: NAR 2024 Profile timing data; industry benchmarks.]
- Monthly GCI contribution from a 10-lead/month seller pipeline at 20% conversion: approximately $37,000/month.
- Annual GCI contribution at this pace: approximately $444,000.

This model illustrates why the north star drives the strategy. Even a 1-lead-per-month improvement in the seller pipeline, sustained across a year, represents approximately $40,000-$45,000 in additional annual GCI at current Bend price levels.

### 5.4 The seller funnel

The seller funnel has five stages. The Q3 strategy addresses each stage:

**Stage 1 - Awareness:** The homeowner is not actively thinking about selling. They consume content (market reports, neighborhood guides) that builds brand familiarity. Ryan Realty content is present in their feed. Actions: weekly market data videos, monthly neighborhood guides, Facebook audience retargeting.

**Stage 2 - Consideration:** The homeowner is aware their home has appreciated and is beginning to consider selling. They search for Bend market data, read articles about equity, or watch videos about the selling process. Actions: SEO blog posts targeting consideration-phase queries ("how much is my Bend home worth 2026"), home valuation tool, retargeting ads for home valuation page visitors.

**Stage 3 - Intent:** The homeowner actively researches selling, compares agents, or requests a home valuation. This is the moment the lead is captured. Actions: home valuation CTA on all content, Facebook lead form ads, GBP "list your home" CTA.

**Stage 4 - Qualification:** The captured lead is assessed. Is this homeowner ready to sell in 12 months? Do they own a property in Deschutes County? Are they open to a listing conversation? Actions: FUB intake sequence, audit-crm SLA monitoring, broker outreach within 24 hours.

**Stage 5 - Listing:** The qualified seller signs a listing agreement with Ryan Realty. Actions: listing agreement workflow, list-kit producer dispatched for photography and content production, listing launch campaign.

The Q3 strategy's primary investment is in Stages 1, 2, and 3. Stage 4 and 5 are operational and scale with the team's capacity (see §16).

## 6. Four buyer and seller personas

Personas are drawn from persona-research.md, bend-market-bible.md, voice_guidelines.md §9, and the NAR 2024 Profile of Home Buyers and Sellers. Every piece of Q3 content is mapped to one or more of these personas.

### 6.1 Persona A: The Out-of-State Seller

**Who they are:**
A former Bend resident or absentee property owner currently living in California, Washington, or Colorado. They purchased their Bend property between 2013 and 2022, most commonly in the $320,000-$500,000 range. They have watched the Bend market from a distance and know that home prices have risen significantly, but they are not sure if now is the right time to sell or if prices will continue rising.

**Demographics:**
Age 48-65. Dual-income or retired. Household income above $120,000. Owns one other primary residence outside Oregon. The Bend property is either a former primary residence, an inherited property, or an investment property. May or may not have visited Bend in the last 24 months.

**The fear that keeps them up at night:**
They will hire the wrong agent, the agent will not show up at the property, the agent will accept a lowball offer because it is the easy path, or the agent will go days without returning a call. The voice_guidelines.md §9 reference seller persona is drawn directly from this profile.

**How they find Ryan Realty:**
Google search for "sell my Bend home," "Bend Oregon real estate agent reviews," or "best brokerage to sell Bend property." YouTube or Facebook content that shows up in their feed when they search Bend real estate topics. A referral from a Bend-based friend or family member.

**What they need to see before calling:**
Evidence that Ryan Realty is physically present in Bend (listing photos, open house announcements, local market knowledge). Evidence that Ryan Realty answers the phone and returns texts (GBP reviews documenting this explicitly). Market data that shows Ryan Realty understands the current Bend pricing environment. Proof of recent successful sales.

**Content that converts this persona:**
- Monthly market report videos showing exact price, days on market, and months of supply for their neighborhood.
- GBP reviews from clients who reference the remote-selling experience.
- A specific blog post: "How to sell your Bend property when you live out of state."
- Facebook ads targeting Bend homeowners by zip code, with the hook "Your Bend home may have earned more equity than you think."

**Q3 content allocation for this persona:** 40% of seller-intent content.

**What the brain emits for this persona:**
Every 30 days, generate-briefs emits one content action targeting Persona A if the north-star metric (qualified_seller_leads_mtd) has not improved by at least 10% over the prior month. The action maps to `content:fb_ad_creative` (Facebook seller ad) or `content:blog_post` (out-of-state seller explainer) depending on which channel is underperforming. Priority_score for Persona A content = 0.85 (highest tier; direct north_star_impact = 1.0, brand_position_lift = 0.5, channel_growth = 0.3).

### 6.2 Persona B: The Equity-Ready Bend Homeowner

**Who they are:**
A long-tenured Bend homeowner who purchased between 2015 and 2021. They bought in the $280,000-$450,000 range and their home is now worth $480,000-$700,000. They have built substantial equity but are not actively thinking about selling. They might sell if they understood how much equity they have, if the market timing felt right, or if a life event (job change, growing family, retirement) created a reason.

**Demographics:**
Age 38-60. Works in Bend (healthcare, education, government, outdoor industry, remote tech). Has lived in the same home for 5-15 years. Knows the neighborhood well. Probably does not follow real estate market data closely.

**How they find Ryan Realty:**
Organic content in their social feed (market data video, neighborhood guide). Facebook neighborhood group. Referral from a neighbor who recently sold. Google search after a life event ("should I sell my house in Bend 2026").

**What converts this persona:**
They need to be educated before they are sold to. Content showing their neighborhood's price trajectory over the last 5 years, in plain language, with their price range specifically. Content showing how Bend's equity accumulation compares to other Oregon markets. The home valuation tool as a low-commitment entry point.

**Content that works:**
- "What $350K bought in Larkspur in 2019, and what that house is worth today" (Facebook or blog).
- Monthly market report with neighborhood-specific breakdowns (NW Crossing, Larkspur, Northpointe).
- Instagram Reel showing year-over-year price appreciation by neighborhood with a CTA to the home valuation tool.
- Email sequence for home valuation submitters from this demographic: 3-touch sequence over 30 days ending with a "ready to talk?" invitation.

**Q3 content allocation:** 30% of seller-intent content.

**What the brain emits:**
Monthly market data video (`content:market_data_short`) always targets this persona implicitly. If the CRM audit shows a cluster of Persona B leads in a specific neighborhood, generate-briefs emits a neighborhood-specific market report (`content:neighborhood_reel`) targeting that area.

### 6.3 Persona C: The Relocating Professional Buyer

**Who they are:**
A remote worker or relocating professional from California, Washington, or Colorado. Has researched Bend for 6-24 months. Is ready to buy but has not settled on a neighborhood or is uncertain about timing. Household income is sufficient for a $500,000-$900,000 purchase. Has visited Bend at least once, possibly multiple times.

**Demographics:**
Age 28-48. Tech, finance, healthcare, or professional services worker. Likely first or second Bend home. Has consumed a significant amount of Bend-related content online before any broker contact. Understands that Bend is popular with their demographic.

**How they find Ryan Realty:**
YouTube or Instagram neighborhood guide. Google search for "best neighborhoods in Bend for families" or "Bend NW Crossing vs Old Bend." Zillow/Redfin listing views that lead to the agent's profile. A referral from a colleague who moved to Bend.

**What converts this persona:**
Depth. This person has done research. They do not need a "Bend is great" pitch. They need specific school data, neighborhood price comparison, employer proximity information, and an honest assessment of what they can get at their price point. Content that treats them as an intelligent adult who has already done Google searches resonates.

**Content that works:**
- YouTube neighborhood comparison videos (NW Crossing vs. Old Bend vs. Larkspur: what you get at $600K).
- Blog posts: "Five questions first-time Bend buyers ask us" (with real answers, not generalities).
- Neighborhood guide series targeting the long-tail queries this persona searches.
- Instagram stories showing the neighborhood morning routine (trail access, coffee shop proximity, school walk).

**Q3 content allocation:** 20% of total content (brand-building with buyer intent, secondary to seller focus).

**What the brain emits:**
Neighborhood guide content is dispatched via `content:neighborhood_reel` (short-form) or `content:area_guide_short` (blog-adjacent). The brain emits neighborhood content when platform-trends or competitor-recon shows a competitor content gap in a specific neighborhood.

### 6.4 Persona D: The STR Investor

**Who they are:**
An investor evaluating or already owning a Bend or Sunriver short-term rental property. Based in California, Oregon, or the Pacific Northwest. Motivated by yield and appreciation. Probably owns at least one other investment property. Has a spreadsheet somewhere.

**Demographics:**
Age 38-60. Higher net worth than the average buyer. Business owner, executive, or professional with investable income above median. Responds to data, not lifestyle photography.

**How they find Ryan Realty:**
Google search ("Sunriver investment property cap rate," "Bend STR ROI"). Referral from another investor. LinkedIn.

Numbers. STR permit status. Annual gross rental income from comparable units. HOA STR allowance or prohibition. Cap rate calculation. Ryan Realty must be very careful here: any income projection must cite actual permit status and actual occupancy data from a verifiable source. No fabricated cap rates.

**Content that works:**
- Blog post: "Sunriver short-term rental: what the 2025 numbers actually show."
- Instagram Reel showing a specific Sunriver property type with the STR income range (verified, sourced, accurate).
- LinkedIn post targeting the investor demographic with market data framing.

**Q3 content allocation:** 10% of total content. This persona is valuable but small.

**What the brain emits:**
generate-briefs emits STR investor content only when a new listing in Sunriver or Tetherow is entering the pipeline, or when a platform-trends signal shows STR investment content performing above average on LinkedIn or YouTube.

## 7. Competitive landscape

Ryan Realty competes with eight named Bend market brokerages and two national disruptors.

### 7.1 Primary local competitors

**Cascade Sotheby's International Realty (Bend):**
The prestige brand in Bend. Affiliated with Sotheby's International and Christie's International Real Estate (marketed separately in some contexts). Strong presence in the $800K+ segment. Visual brand is aspirational and lifestyle-first. Content strategy: professional photography-heavy, minimal data content, moderate social presence. Key strength: brand recognition among luxury buyers. Key weakness: data-light positioning gives Ryan Realty a clear differentiation lane.

**Hasson Company Realtors (Portland-Bend):**
Portland-based brokerage with a Bend presence. Strong in the NW Crossing and NW Bend segments where Portland buyers are common. Content strategy: blog-forward, reasonably data-driven, but produced at lower velocity than Ryan Realty's target. Key weakness: not a dedicated Bend firm; local market depth is a vulnerability.

**John L. Scott Real Estate (Bend):**
National franchise with local agents. Moderate Bend presence. Content strategy: franchise templates, variable quality by agent, no distinctive data voice. Key weakness: franchise marketing feels generic in a market that rewards local specificity.

**Compass (Bend):**
National tech-enabled brokerage with a Bend presence. Technology tools are strong at the agent level but brand marketing at the local level is minimal. Key strength: agent tools. Key weakness: no local brand identity.

**EXIT Realty Bend:**
Smaller local operation. Limited social presence. Content strategy: minimal. Key weakness: below-average digital presence.

**Coldwell Banker Sun Country:**
Franchise with Bend agents. Moderate local presence. Content strategy: franchise templates. Key weakness: franchise generic positioning.

**RE/MAX Key Properties:**
Franchise with established local agents. Some individual agents have strong personal brands within the brokerage. Content strategy: variable by agent. Key weakness: no brokerage-level content differentiation.

**Berkshire Hathaway HomeServices (BHHS) NW Real Estate:**
National brand with local Bend presence. Strong in the mid-market. Key weakness: national franchise brand does not carry the local specificity that Ryan Realty can build.

### 7.2 National disruptors

**Redfin:** Technology-driven, lower-commission model. Targets price-sensitive sellers. Ryan Realty's counter-positioning: full-service expertise produces better outcomes that justify standard commission. The content strategy builds this case without naming Redfin.

**Opendoor/iBuyers:** Cash offer models. Appeal to sellers who want speed over maximizing price. Ryan Realty's counter-positioning: data transparency shows what sellers give up in net proceeds when they take a below-market cash offer. The market report content supports this implicitly.

### 7.3 Competitive positioning matrix

| Data transparency | High | Low | Medium | Low |
| Local market depth | High | High | Medium | Variable |

## 22. The content loop compounding model

The Reforge framework (framework-references.md §1) identifies the content loop as a compounding asset. This section models how Ryan Realty's Q3 content investment compounds.

**The compounding assumption:**
Each piece of content (blog post, YouTube video, Instagram Reel) reaches an audience immediately on publication, then continues to reach new audiences through search, recommendation, and shares over time.

A blog post targeting "how to sell your Bend home from out of state" published in July 2026 may generate:
- 50-200 visits in its first month from social distribution and email newsletter links.
- 500-2,000 visits over 12 months from organic search as it accumulates backlinks and climbs the rankings.
- 2,000-8,000 visits over 36 months as rankings solidify and compound.

At a 2% home-valuation-tool completion rate (conservative), that 36-month traffic generates 40-160 home valuation submissions from a single blog post. At a 20% qualification rate, that is 8-32 qualified seller leads from one $0-spend content asset.

**The Q3 content library adds 6 blog posts, 3 long-form YouTube videos, 3 neighborhood guides, 12 market data videos, and approximately 100 social posts to the library.**

Not all of these assets compound equally. Long-form blog posts and YouTube videos compound the most (search authority). Short-form Reels have a 72-hour peak window and then decay. The Q3 content strategy weights investment toward the compounding assets (blog + YouTube) while maintaining the Reels cadence for brand presence.

**The compounding model for brand authority:**
Brand authority (measured by branded search volume and share of voice on Bend real estate queries) compounds differently from traffic. Each new piece of content that Ryan Realty publishes signals to Google that the site is actively maintained. Each new backlink (earned by publishable market reports) raises domain authority. Each GBP review adds to the trust signal. All three accumulate non-linearly. By end of Q3, Ryan Realty should have a meaningfully higher domain authority and branded search volume than at Q3 start.
