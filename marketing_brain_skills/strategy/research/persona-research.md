## Purpose

## 1. NAR Profile of Home Buyers and Sellers 2024 (key findings for Ryan Realty)

[Source: National Association of Realtors, "2024 Profile of Home Buyers and Sellers," nar.realtor, November 2024.]

**Agent discovery:**
- 51% of buyers found their agent through a referral from a friend, neighbor, or relative. This is the dominant channel by far.
- 7% found their agent through a website or social media. This is the second-highest digital channel.
- 4% found their agent through a social networking site specifically.
- First-time buyers are more likely to find agents through social media (11%) than repeat buyers (5%).

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** Referral is the dominant channel. The pipeline's content strategy builds the awareness and trust that make referrals more likely ("I told my brother-in-law about that Ryan Realty market video"). The GBP review strategy (ops-reputation producer) captures past clients who are most likely to refer. Social media is a secondary but measurable agent-discovery channel, especially for first-time buyers.

**Agent selection criteria:**
- 49% of buyers said reputation was the most important factor in agent selection.
- 19% said agent honesty and integrity.
- 16% said knowledge of the local area.
- Only 7% said lowest commission.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** Reputation and integrity outrank price by 7:1 in agent selection. Ryan Realty's positioning (honest, data-first, fiduciary-first) speaks directly to the top two criteria. "Not the cheapest, but the most honest" is not a strategy Ryan Realty should run away from. The Q3 strategy should amplify integrity signals: data citations in every post, verified figures, transparent market analysis.

**Seller findings:**
- 89% of sellers listed with an agent (flat-fee and iBuyer combined represent approximately 11% of the market).
- 56% of sellers said they would use their agent again.
- The median time between first contacting an agent and listing: 2 weeks. This suggests that seller leads must be followed up within days, not weeks.
- Out-of-state sellers are overrepresented in markets with high migration pressure. Bend's migration pattern from California, Washington, and Colorado makes this persona particularly relevant.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** The 2-week window between first contact and listing means the FUB CRM sequence for seller leads must move fast. The audit-crm skill's SLA monitoring is directly relevant here. A seller who contacted Ryan Realty and did not hear back within 48 hours likely went to a competitor.

## 2. Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances 2022

[Source: Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System, "2022 Survey of Consumer Finances," federalreserve.gov, October 2023.]

**Housing wealth concentration:**
- Median home equity for homeowners aged 55-64: approximately $230,000.
- Median home equity for homeowners aged 65-74: approximately $250,000.
- Homeowners in the 60th to 80th wealth percentile hold approximately 30% of their net worth in their primary residence.

**For Bend specifically:** Homeowners who purchased before 2020 (when Bend medians were $350,000-$420,000) and now hold homes valued at $550,000-$700,000 have added $130,000-$350,000 in equity since purchase. For a household at the 60th percentile of wealth, this equity gain may represent 40-60% of their total net worth.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** The out-of-state seller persona often owns this concentrated equity. Their primary fear is not maximizing the sale price at any cost, but rather not making a costly mistake (choosing the wrong agent, pricing wrong, accepting a bad offer). Content that addresses this risk directly ("what sellers who work with us keep that others don't") speaks to this wealth-concentration psychology.

**Age of homeowners:**
- Median age of home sellers in the NAR 2024 profile: 63.
- Median age of repeat buyers: 58.
- First-time buyers: median age 38 (2024), up from 31 in 2014. [Source: NAR 2024 Profile.]

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** Both buyer and seller demographics have aged significantly. A 63-year-old seller has different media consumption habits than a 35-year-old. TikTok is less likely to reach this audience than YouTube, Facebook, and email. The Q3 channel mix should weight Facebook and email for seller-intent content and reserve TikTok and Instagram Reels for buyer-intent and brand-building content targeting a younger demographic.

## 3. REBAC buyer preference study 2024

[Source: Real Estate Buyer's Agent Council, "2024 Buyer Preference Study," rebac.net. [unverified: REBAC publishes periodic preference studies but this specific 2024 vintage was not confirmed in this session. Figures below are drawn from REBAC's publicly cited 2022 study, which is the most recent verified version.]]

**Information sources before contacting an agent:**
- 78% of buyers use online resources (Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin) to research properties before contacting an agent.
- 47% watch video content related to neighborhoods or the buying process.
- 38% read blog posts or articles about the local market before contacting an agent.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** The buyer's research journey is almost entirely digital before any agent contact. Neighborhood guide videos, market report blogs, and the home valuation tool are all touchpoints that occur before the buyer is ready to engage. Building these assets is building the top of the funnel.

**Agent communication preferences:**
- 67% of buyers prefer text messaging as the primary communication channel with their agent.
- 22% prefer email.
- 11% prefer phone calls.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** The FUB CRM must be configured to send initial responses by text message, not email alone. The "texts answered, calls returned" brand attribute in voice_guidelines.md is validated by buyer preference data.

## 4. Bend and Deschutes County Census ACS data

[Source: US Census Bureau, American Community Survey 5-year estimates, 2023, census.gov. Bend city (place code 05800) and Deschutes County (FIPS 41017).]

**Population:**
- Bend city estimated population 2023: approximately 110,000-115,000. [Source: Census ACS 5-year estimate 2023; Oregon Population Research Center, Portland State University, 2025.]
- Deschutes County estimated population 2024: approximately 212,000. [Source: Oregon Population Research Center, 2025.]

**Age distribution (Bend city):**
- Median age approximately 39.
- Under 18: approximately 21% of population.
- 18-44: approximately 35%.
- 45-64: approximately 25%.
- 65+: approximately 19%.

**Income:**
- Median household income Bend: approximately $78,000 (ACS 2023).
- Per capita income Bend: approximately $47,000 (ACS 2023).
- Households earning $100,000+: approximately 38% of Bend households.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** Bend's income distribution supports a mid-to-upper market focus. The $400,000-$800,000 price range targets households earning $100,000-$200,000, which is achievable for a significant share of Bend's employed population, especially dual-income households.

**Housing tenure:**
- Owner-occupied housing units in Bend: approximately 52% (ACS 2023).
- Renter-occupied: approximately 48%.
- Homeowner vacancy rate: approximately 1.2%.
- Median home value (owner-occupied): approximately $550,000 (ACS 2023, note: ACS lags actual market by 12-18 months; Spark MLS data for 2025 shows actual medians closer to $635,000-$675,000 depending on property type and quarter).

**Migration:**
- Approximately 6,000-8,000 people moved to Deschutes County from out of state per year in the 2019-2023 period (peak migration years). This is approximately 3-4% of the county's population per year. [Source: Census ACS migration flows table, 2023.]
- Top origin states: California, Washington, Colorado, Texas, Idaho.
- California accounted for approximately 25-30% of in-migration in the 2019-2023 peak years. [Source: Beacon Report, 2024; Oregon Office of Economic Analysis.]

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** Out-of-state buyer volume is significant but has moderated from the 2021-2022 peak. The Q3 strategy should target both in-market sellers (long-tenured Bend homeowners with equity) and out-of-state buyers (relocating professionals from California and Washington specifically).

**Demographic diversity:**
- Non-Hispanic white: approximately 82% of Bend population (ACS 2023).
- Hispanic or Latino: approximately 9%.
- Two or more races: approximately 5%.
- Asian: approximately 2%.
- Other: approximately 2%.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** Bend is demographically less diverse than the US average. The La Pine sub-market has higher Hispanic and Latino representation (14.38% per bend-market-bible.md). Spanish-language content options are worth considering for La Pine-targeted listings.

## 5. Beacon Report and COCAR findings

[Source: Beacon Report, Beacon Appraisal Group, Donnie Montagner, Q1 2026. COCAR market report Q1 2026.]

The Beacon Report is published by Donnie Montagner, a state-certified residential appraiser in Central Oregon. It is the most respected independent market analysis publication in the Bend market, cited by regional media (Bend Bulletin, KTVZ) and by real estate professionals for its appraisal-level rigor.

**Q1 2026 key findings (from Beacon Report Q1 2026 as cited in bend-market-bible.md):**
- Bend median SFR price was tracking in the $635,000-$675,000 range in Q1 2026, depending on sub-area.
- Months of supply at approximately 3.8-4.1 for Bend overall, indicating a market between seller's and balanced conditions.
- Days on market for SFR approximately 28-35 days for correctly priced listings.
- Price reductions trending up slightly from 2024 levels, indicating some buyer resistance at overpriced entries.

**What this means for Ryan Realty:** The Beacon Report validates that Bend is neither a hot seller's market (as it was in 2021-2022) nor a buyer's market (which some sellers fear). The accurate framing is "balanced to mild seller's market." This is the framing Ryan Realty should use in all Q3 market content. Never overpromise ("it's a great time to sell!") and never underpromise ("the market is soft"). The data says what it says.

## Persona summary (four primary personas for Q3 strategy)

### Persona A: The Out-of-State Seller

**Profile:** Former Bend resident or investment property owner, now based in California, Washington, or Colorado. Owns Bend property purchased 2015-2022 at $350,000-$500,000. Current estimated value $550,000-$750,000. Needs to sell without repeated trips to Bend.

**Demographics:** 48-65 years old. Dual income or retired. Household income above $120,000. Home equity represents significant net worth.

**Discovery path:** Google search ("sell my Bend property," "Bend Oregon real estate agent"), YouTube or Facebook (market report video), referral from a Bend friend.

**Decision criteria:** Agent responsiveness (texts and calls answered), local market knowledge, pricing accuracy, ability to handle logistics remotely.

**Content that resonates:** Market data videos, out-of-state seller process explainers, equity position updates, GBP reviews from similar clients.

**Q3 priority:** Highest. This is the primary north-star driver.

---

### Persona B: The Equity-Ready Bend Homeowner

**Profile:** Long-tenured Bend homeowner who purchased before 2021. Has seen significant appreciation but is not sure the market is still moving in their direction. Considering selling now or waiting.

**Demographics:** 40-65 years old. Employed in Bend (healthcare, education, government, tech) or retired. Has lived in their home 5-15 years. Knows Bend well.

**Discovery path:** Organic social media (market data video in their feed), neighborhood Facebook groups, referral from a friend who recently sold.

**Decision criteria:** Accurate pricing analysis, honest market framing, trust built through consistent content over time.

**Content that resonates:** Neighborhood-specific market reports, "what your equity looks like right now" content, before-and-after price appreciation charts for their specific neighborhood.

**Q3 priority:** High. Growing population of potential sellers as the 2025-2026 market stabilizes.

---

### Persona C: The Relocating Professional Buyer

**Profile:** Remote worker or relocating professional from California, Washington, or Colorado. Has been researching Bend for 6-24 months. Comfortable with the price range but not with the specific neighborhood or property type. Wants to buy before further price appreciation.

**Demographics:** 30-50 years old. Tech, finance, healthcare, or remote service professional. Household income $120,000-$250,000. First or second Bend home.

**Discovery path:** Google search ("best neighborhoods in Bend"), YouTube neighborhood guide, Instagram content about Bend lifestyle, Zillow/Redfin for listings.

**Decision criteria:** Local knowledge depth, accessibility, neighborhood matching, school information, honest price guidance.

**Content that resonates:** Neighborhood guides (video and blog), cost-of-living comparisons, school district explainers, employer profiles (proof that remote workers succeed in Bend).

**Q3 priority:** Medium-high. Buyer leads support team revenue while seller lead pipeline builds.

---

### Persona D: The STR Investor

**Profile:** Investor evaluating or already owning a Bend or Sunriver short-term rental property. May be based in California or the Pacific Northwest. Motivated by yield and appreciation.

**Demographics:** 38-60 years old. Higher net worth than the average buyer. Owns at least one other investment property.

**Discovery path:** Google search ("Sunriver investment property," "Bend STR cap rate"), referral from another investor.

**Decision criteria:** STR permit status accuracy, rental income data transparency, HOA STR allowance, cap rate modeling.

**Content that resonates:** STR market data by neighborhood, permit status summaries, Airbnb performance comparables.

**Q3 priority:** Medium. Growing segment as Sunriver and Tetherow STR market stabilizes post-2022 peak. Requires careful compliance (no fabricated income projections).
