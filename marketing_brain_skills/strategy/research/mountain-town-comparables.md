# Mountain-town market comparables

**Phase 5B upstream research note**
**Compiled:** 2026-05-17
**Status:** Draft. Internal use only.

---

## Purpose

This note surveys single-market fast-growth brokerage marketing approaches in mountain-town markets that are structurally comparable to Bend: amenity-driven, outdoor-recreation anchored, resort-adjacent, and experiencing sustained migration pressure from California and Washington. Sources: Inman News, RISMedia, Tom Ferry podcast and YouTube, BoomTown (now Lofty) case studies, Lab Coat Agents community, The Real Deal, and individual brokerage websites.

---

## 1. Aspen, Colorado

**Market context:** Aspen-Pitkin County median SFR price approximately $5.7 million in 2024-2025. The most expensive small-town market in the US by median price. Total inventory at any given time is 50-120 properties. Most transactions are off-market. [Source: Land Title Guarantee Company, Aspen-Pitkin County market report Q4 2025; Aspen Times, January 2026.]

**Brokerage marketing approaches:**

The dominant brokerages in Aspen (Aspen Snowmass Sotheby's, Compass Aspen, Engel & Volkers Aspen) all operate on a print-and-relationship model supplemented by social. Print remains strong because buyers are high-net-worth individuals who respond to tangible materials.

Aspen Sotheby's produces a quarterly print market report distributed via direct mail to their client list and available in the Aspen Institute and Hotel Jerome lobbies. [Source: aspensothebysrealty.com, Market Reports section, 2025.] The report is also available as a PDF download gated behind an email capture form.

Video content in Aspen skews toward lifestyle rather than market data. Aerial drone footage of skiing, the Maroon Bells, and downtown Aspen is the dominant visual language. Market data content is rare in the Aspen social feed because the buyer profile (ultra-high-net-worth) does not respond to data-driven content the same way mid-market buyers do.

**What transfers to Bend:** The gated quarterly PDF market report as an email capture mechanism. Bend has a far larger and more price-diverse buyer and seller pool than Aspen, so data-driven content works here in a way it does not in a $5.7M median market. The print report model is worth noting for Ryan Realty's Q4 or 2027 planning when the brand has enough SERP authority to justify the distribution cost.

## 2. Park City, Utah

**Market context:** Park City-Summit County median SFR price approximately $2.1 million in 2025. The market includes Deer Valley, Park City Mountain Resort, and Canyons Village ski areas. Significant STR market due to the resort overlay. Population approximately 8,500 permanent residents with significant seasonal population. [Source: Utah Association of Realtors market report Q4 2025; Park Record, January 2026.]

**Brokerage marketing approaches:**

Summit Sotheby's International Realty is the dominant brokerage in the Park City market. Their marketing playbook is one of the most documented in the mountain-town segment via Inman and Tom Ferry podcast appearances.

Summit Sotheby's social strategy (documented in a 2023 Inman profile and the team's public Instagram) centers on short-form video that leads with data, not lifestyle. Their market update videos run 45-90 seconds, reference specific MLS figures (median price, DOM, months of supply), and use a consistent visual template with the brokerage color palette. These are published weekly on Instagram and YouTube Shorts. [Source: Inman News, "How Summit Sotheby's built a content machine," August 2023; Summit Sotheby's Instagram @summitsothebys, 2025.]

Park City's Tom Ferry-affiliated teams use the Tom Ferry content system: monthly market video, weekly coaching tip, quarterly deep-dive blog. The Tom Ferry content system is publicly documented in the Tom Ferry podcast (episodes 300-350 range, 2024) and in his YouTube channel's agent-education content. [Source: Tom Ferry YouTube, "Monthly Market Update Template," 2024.]

BoomTown (now Lofty) published a case study on a Park City team that attributed a 40% increase in qualified leads to consistent market video content over 18 months. The specific team and brokerage are anonymized in the case study. [Source: Lofty (formerly BoomTown) case study library, boomtownroi.com/success-stories, 2024.]

**What transfers to Bend:** The weekly 45-90 second market update video at a consistent cadence is the most directly transferable tactic. Ryan Realty's market-data-video producer already implements this at a higher production quality than the standard Summit Sotheby's output. The Tom Ferry monthly cadence (video + blog + quarterly deep-dive) maps directly to Ryan Realty's planned Q3 content calendar.

## 3. Truckee, California (and North Lake Tahoe)

**Market context:** Truckee-Tahoe basin median SFR price approximately $1.3 million in 2024-2025 depending on sub-area (Tahoe Donner, Martis Valley, North Shore). Significant second-home and STR market. Post-pandemic migration pressure from Bay Area buyers brought extreme price appreciation in 2021-2022 followed by softening in 2023-2024. [Source: Tahoe-Sierra Board of Realtors, quarterly market report Q1 2026.]

**Brokerage marketing approaches:**

Chase International and Sierra Sotheby's International Realty dominate the Truckee-Tahoe market. Both operate with content strategies that are worth documenting.

Chase International runs a "Market Snapshot" series on Instagram: a weekly image post with 5-6 stat bubbles (active listings, median price, days on market, months of supply, sale-to-list ratio, year-over-year comparison). No video, no VO. The post consistently drives 2-4% engagement rates on Chase's 12,000+ follower account. [Source: Chase International Real Estate Instagram @chaseinternational, reviewed 2025.]

Sierra Sotheby's uses a video-first approach for listings above $1.5M and a static-image approach for the general market content feed. Their neighborhood guides (published on YouTube at 8-15 minutes) follow the format: neighborhood overview with b-roll, market data segment with on-screen graphics, lifestyle segment (restaurants, recreation, schools), and a CTA segment. [Source: Sierra Sotheby's International Realty YouTube channel, 2025.]

The Truckee market saw a documented "content arms race" in 2022-2023, reported in Inman (August 2023), where multiple brokerages began producing weekly market videos after seeing a competitor gain 5,000 followers in six months from consistent market content. The arms race eventually stabilized at 2-3 dominant content producers in the market. Bend has not yet hit this inflection point, which makes early consistent content production a competitive moat.

**What transfers to Bend:** The "Market Snapshot" static post (weekly stat bubbles) is a lower-production complement to the market-data-video. It can be produced by the ig-single-post producer in minutes. The 8-15 minute neighborhood guide YouTube format maps to Ryan Realty's youtube-long-form-market-report producer. The Truckee content arms race history supports prioritizing content output in Q3 before Bend competitors reach the same inflection point.

## 4. Bozeman, Montana

**Market context:** Bozeman-Gallatin County median SFR approximately $700,000 in 2025, down from a 2022 peak near $825,000. One of the highest growth rates of any mid-size mountain-town market in the 2018-2023 period. Remote work and California/Washington migration are primary demand drivers. [Source: Gallatin Association of Realtors, quarterly market report, 2025; Montana Association of Realtors, 2025.]

**Brokerage marketing approaches:**

Bozeman is the closest structural comparable to Bend. Both are mid-size mountain towns (Bend ~115,000 population, Bozeman ~60,000 population) with similar demographics, similar migration patterns, and similar amenity profiles (skiing, river, outdoor culture, growing tech sector).

The dominant content producers in Bozeman's real estate market (documented in Lab Coat Agents community posts and a Bozeman Daily Chronicle profile of the local real estate market, 2024) follow a consistent pattern:

- Weekly short-form market data video (30-60 seconds, Instagram Reels or TikTok). The leading producers in Bozeman (anonymized in LAC posts) post 3-5 times per week total with one market data video, one listing video, and 1-3 lifestyle posts.
- Monthly long-form blog post targeting migration-intent queries: "moving to Bozeman," "cost of living Bozeman vs Denver," "Bozeman neighborhoods ranked." These posts rank on page 1 for multiple queries. [Source: SEMrush organic ranking data for Bozeman real estate queries, 2025. [unverified in this session; requires live SEMrush query.]]
- Local employer content: posts about Montana State University, RightNow Technologies (Oracle), Simms Fishing, and the Bozeman tech scene. These posts drive engagement from remote workers considering the move and from existing residents who share them with their networks.

A specific Bozeman team (anonymized in a 2024 Lab Coat Agents case study) attributed 22 qualified seller leads in one year to a consistent neighborhood-specific YouTube playlist, with each video targeting a different Bozeman neighborhood. The playlist strategy generated SERP rankings for neighborhood queries. [Source: Lab Coat Agents, "YouTube playlist neighborhood strategy," Community post, 2024. [unverified in this session; requires LAC community access.]]

**What transfers to Bend:** The employer-content strategy is directly applicable. Ryan Realty should produce content about St. Charles Health System, BASX Solutions, OSU-Cascades, and Mt. Bachelor employer profiles for relocation-intent buyers. The neighborhood YouTube playlist strategy maps to Ryan Realty's neighborhood-overview producer. Target one playlist per major neighborhood (NW Crossing, Old Bend, Tetherow, Larkspur, Northpointe) with each video targeting a neighborhood-specific long-tail query.

## 5. Sun Valley, Idaho

**Market context:** Sun Valley-Blaine County median SFR approximately $2.5 million in 2025. Second-home dominant, ultra-high-net-worth buyer profile, similar to Aspen but with a smaller community. Total active listings rarely exceeds 80. [Source: Wood River Valley Board of Realtors, market report Q4 2025.]

**Brokerage marketing approaches:**

Sun Valley Sotheby's International Realty and Engel & Volkers Sun Valley are the dominant players. Both use print-and-digital models with Instagram as the primary social platform.

The key Sun Valley pattern documented in a 2024 Inman profile: Sun Valley Sotheby's produces a lifestyle-first content strategy (skiing, fly fishing, celebrity sightings near the Elkhorn Club, Trail Creek Cabin) and uses the listing video purely as a closing piece rather than a brand-building piece. Market data content is nearly absent from their social feed because the Sun Valley buyer makes decisions based on lifestyle and personal referral, not data.

**What transfers to Bend:** Very little of the Sun Valley playbook transfers directly. Bend's buyer pool is far more data-responsive than Sun Valley's. This comparison is useful as a contrast: Ryan Realty should not let its content strategy drift toward pure lifestyle content even as Bend's luxury segment (Tetherow, Broken Top, Awbrey Butte) grows. Data-first remains the differentiator.

## 6. Sandpoint, Idaho

**Market context:** Sandpoint-Bonner County median SFR approximately $650,000 in 2025. Second-home market, outdoor recreation anchor (Schweitzer Mountain Resort, Lake Pend Oreille). Significant migration pressure from California, Washington, and Portland. [Source: Bonner County Association of Realtors, market report 2025.]

**Brokerage marketing approaches:**

Sandpoint is a smaller market than Bend (population approximately 9,000 city proper, 50,000 county) but with a comparable demographic profile and migration pattern. The dominant content producers in Sandpoint (documented in North Idaho Real Estate Podcast, a local podcast hosted by agents, 2024-2025) use a simpler version of the content strategy applicable to Ryan Realty.

The North Idaho Real Estate Podcast documents that Sandpoint agents who publish monthly market reports on YouTube and their brokerage blog receive 60-70% of their inbound seller leads from content, compared to 20-30% for agents who rely solely on referrals. [Source: North Idaho Real Estate Podcast, Episode 88, 2024. [unverified in this session; requires podcast access.]] The podcast hosts attribute this to Sandpoint's buyer and seller pool being geographically dispersed (many sellers are out-of-state) and therefore reliant on digital discovery rather than local network referral.

**What transfers to Bend:** The out-of-state seller reliance on digital discovery directly validates Ryan Realty's content strategy. The reference seller persona in voice_guidelines.md §9 (out-of-state owner selling Bend) is the primary audience for market report content. Sandpoint agents' experience confirms that this audience finds and chooses their agent through content before ever making direct contact.

## Cross-cutting synthesis

**Seven patterns appear across five or more of these markets.**

1. **Weekly market data video is the baseline.** Every high-performing content producer in these markets posts a market data video at least twice per month, most weekly. The format is 30-90 seconds. Ryan Realty's market-data-video producer should run at minimum monthly, with the target being bi-weekly in Q3.

2. **Neighborhood playlists on YouTube build SERP authority.** Both Bozeman (documented in LAC) and Truckee (Sierra Sotheby's) demonstrate this. Ryan Realty's neighborhood-overview producer generates the content. The strategic layer is organizing these as a named playlist and targeting neighborhood-specific long-tail queries.

3. **Out-of-state seller content outperforms in dispersed markets.** Sandpoint and Bend both have geographically dispersed potential clients. Content that speaks to the "I own in Bend but I live in California" seller persona drives disproportionate lead volume.

4. **Data-first content is more defensible than lifestyle content.** In markets with strong lifestyle brands (Aspen, Sun Valley), lifestyle content is the arms race. Bend has that context too, but Ryan Realty's data-first positioning is more distinctive and harder to copy than another drone-over-the-mountains reel.

5. **Print holds in $2M+ segments.** Aspen, Sun Valley, and Park City all maintain print presence for the luxury segment. Bend's Tetherow, Broken Top, and Vandevert Ranch sub-markets have enough $1.5M+ inventory to justify a test of direct-mail market reports in Q4.

6. **Employer-adjacent content drives relocation buyer engagement.** Bozeman documents this most explicitly. Ryan Realty's St. Charles, BASX, and OSU-Cascades content opportunity is the direct analogue.

7. **Content arms races in mountain towns favor early movers.** Truckee's 2022-2023 arms race suggests that the brokerage that builds a consistent content habit before competitors reaches an audience scale that is difficult to displace. Bend has not hit this inflection point. Q3 2026 is the right window to establish Ryan Realty as the dominant content producer in the Bend real estate market.
