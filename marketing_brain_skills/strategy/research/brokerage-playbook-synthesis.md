# Brokerage playbook synthesis

**Phase 5B upstream research note**
**Compiled:** 2026-05-17
**Status:** Draft. Internal use only. Not a public-facing deliverable. Voice rules apply per voice_guidelines.md.

---

## Purpose

This note synthesizes how eight major US brokerage models structure their marketing playbooks. The goal is to extract patterns that a single-market independent brokerage like Ryan Realty can legitimately adopt, adapt, or consciously reject. Every observation below traces to a public source: investor decks, annual reports, Inman News, RISMedia, The Real Deal, or published agent-recruiting materials.

---

## 1. Compass

**Model:** Technology-enabled residential brokerage. IPO'd 2021 (NYSE: COMP). 30,000+ agents across 70+ markets as of 2025. Operates on a high split model (agents keep 80-100% after reaching a cap) funded by venture capital and debt. [Source: Compass 2024 Annual Report, SEC 10-K filing.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- Compass agents receive access to a proprietary CRM (Compass CRM), marketing center (Compass Concierge), and listing management platform. These tools are Compass's agent-retention mechanism, not primarily a client-acquisition mechanism.
- Brand marketing is largely absent at the national level. Compass relies on individual agent brand-building rather than a unified brokerage brand.
- "Compass Concierge" fronts the cost of pre-listing improvements (paint, staging, minor repairs) with no upfront cost to the seller. This is both a lead-generation tool and a differentiated listing service. [Source: Compass.com Concierge program page, 2025.]
- Coming Soon listings created through Compass's own platform give Compass listings a brief exclusive window before MLS syndication. This draws both buyers and sellers into the Compass ecosystem.
- Marketing spend is driven to agent tools rather than brand advertising. Compass does not run broad consumer brand campaigns.

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** The Compass playbook is primarily an agent-recruitment and agent-retention play. For a principal-broker-led single-market firm, the lesson is narrower: Concierge-style pre-listing services can be a lead differentiator without requiring Compass's capital structure. Ryan Realty can offer contractor coordination and pre-listing consultation as a service differentiation without fronting cash.

## 2. eXp World Holdings

**Model:** Cloud-brokerage, asset-light. Publicly traded (NASDAQ: EXPI). No physical offices; agents work via a virtual 3D environment called eXp World. Agents receive an 80/20 split with a $16,000 cap and stock-based compensation tied to agent referrals. Over 80,000 agents globally as of 2025. [Source: eXp World Holdings 2024 Annual Report, SEC 10-K.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- eXp's marketing playbook is built around agent social media activity, not brokerage brand advertising. The playbook is documented in eXp University (internal training library) and through public agent-authored YouTube content.
- Agent-generated video content (market updates, buyer and seller education, neighborhood guides) is the primary lead-generation channel. eXp provides templates, stock footage licenses, and training through eXp University.
- The eXp model explicitly values content quantity over individual video production quality. Agents are trained to post daily or near-daily video, not to perfect each video.
- Team structures within eXp are the growth unit. Team leaders recruit sub-agents and earn revenue share on those agents' transactions. Marketing for team leaders is a recruiting pitch as much as a consumer pitch.
- eXp's national brand is essentially a tech-forward value proposition for agent recruitment, not a consumer brand.

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** The eXp model validates content volume as a channel strategy, but the quality-over-quantity approach Ryan Realty takes is a deliberate counter-position. In a single market where every potential client knows other clients, one substantive weekly market video builds more trust than seven rushed daily posts. The eXp playbook is a useful counterpoint: Ryan Realty's differentiation is depth and accuracy, not output velocity.

## 3. The Agency

**Model:** Boutique luxury real estate brokerage. Founded 2011 by Mauricio Umansky. Private company. Approximately 1,000 agents across 50+ offices, heavily weighted toward California and mountain resort markets (Aspen, Park City, Sun Valley). [Source: The Agency Real Estate website, About page, 2025; Inman News coverage, 2025.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- The Agency's playbook is built on visual brand consistency and celebrity-adjacent positioning. Brand colors (white and black), clean photography standards, and a consistent editorial visual language are enforced across all offices.
- Agent-specific social media guidelines are distributed in The Agency's internal Agent Marketing Guide (not public, but referenced by Inman and multiple agent interviews from 2023-2025).
- Monthly content calendar with property-specific templates, market-specific data drops, and lifestyle content (restaurants, design, culture in the local market).
- Print presence is maintained deliberately as a luxury signal: private client magazines, property brochures on premium paper stocks. [Source: The Agency Real Estate Blog, "Our Marketing Philosophy," 2024.]
- Social strategy emphasizes Instagram and YouTube, with a secondary presence on LinkedIn for agent-to-agent network building.
- The Agency produces a public market report for major markets quarterly. These reports are distributed via email and social, function as lead capture, and build SERP authority for market-data queries.

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** The Agency validates the quarterly or monthly market report as a brand-building and SERP tool. The visual consistency enforcement model is relevant: Ryan Realty's v2 design system (navy on cream, Amboqia Boriango display type, Geist body) serves the same function as The Agency's white-and-black enforcement. The lifestyle content layer (local restaurants, design, events) is a possible content extension Ryan Realty could test in a future quarter.

## 4. Side

**Model:** White-label brokerage infrastructure. Side does not recruit agents under the Side brand. Instead, agents build their own branded boutique brokerage (e.g., "Smith & Co. Real Estate") using Side's back-office, compliance, licensing, and technology stack. [Source: Side.com, How it Works, 2025; Inman News, "Inside Side's model," 2024.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- Side provides no consumer brand marketing. Each partner brokerage markets under its own brand. Side's value proposition is entirely B2B: superior technology, compliance support, transaction management, and agent equity participation.
- Partner brokerages (Side clients) are responsible for their own marketing playbooks. Side provides playbook frameworks and coaching through its agent success team, but implementation is per-brokerage.
- Side's playbook for partner brokerages emphasizes: (a) building a distinct local brand identity rather than a national brokerage identity, (b) content authority in the specific submarket the partner serves, (c) team culture as a recruiting differentiator.
- Side explicitly coaches against the "franchise" aesthetic: no partner brokerage is allowed to look or feel like a Side sub-brand. The whole point is market distinctiveness.

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** The Side model validates Ryan Realty's instinct to build a distinct Bend identity rather than affiliate with a national brand. The "content authority in the specific submarket" coaching is exactly what Ryan Realty's neighborhood-specific market report and listing-area content strategy implements.

## 5. Real Brokerage

**Model:** Tech-enabled cloud brokerage. Publicly traded (NASDAQ: REAX). Revenue share model similar to eXp. Agent count approaching 20,000 as of 2025. [Source: Real Brokerage 2024 Annual Report.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- Real's marketing playbook is nearly entirely agent-driven social media content. The company provides Leo AI (an internal AI assistant for agents) and a content creation app called Lofty (acquired 2024) that generates social posts, property descriptions, and email templates.
- Real's public brand marketing is minimal. The company's brand equity is built through agent word-of-mouth and recruiting.
- Real provides agent-level analytics through its app: views, leads, conversion by content type. Agents are trained to iterate based on data rather than intuition.

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** Real's Lofty acquisition validates the market need for AI-assisted content production in residential real estate. Ryan Realty's autonomous pipeline implements a more sophisticated version of the same concept (AI-drafted content with human brand-voice enforcement rather than unchecked AI output). The agent-level analytics loop Real provides at the individual level is what Ryan Realty's performance_loop implements at the brokerage level.

## 6. Serhant

**Model:** Ryan Serhant's personal-brand-anchored brokerage. Founded 2020. Approximately 700+ agents as of 2025. Known for its production values on social media, a formal media company (S.MEDIA) embedded within the brokerage, and a YouTube channel with 1M+ subscribers. [Source: Serhant.com About page, 2025; Ryan Serhant YouTube channel, 2025.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- Serhant is the most explicit example of a brokerage built on a personal brand. The playbook is documented in Ryan Serhant's own public writing, including his books "Sell It Like Serhant" and "Big Money Energy," his podcast, and Serhant's agent training platform.
- The brokerage runs a formal content team (S.MEDIA) with videographers, editors, and social strategists embedded in-house. Every listing above a threshold receives a full production package.
- Listing videos are produced at near-broadcast quality for high-end properties. S.MEDIA produces the content; the agent does not self-produce.
- Serhant's public market updates are short-form vertical video, primarily on Instagram Reels and TikTok. They are produced at the quality level of a media company, not at the selfie-video level most agents operate at.
- The agent training program at Serhant explicitly teaches content creation as a core agent skill, not a marketing department function.
- Serhant's playbook document (partially public via his training program) breaks content into three tiers: everyday content (shot on phone, posted same day), elevated content (light post-production, weekly), and flagship content (full S.MEDIA production, monthly or per-listing).

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** The three-tier content model (everyday / elevated / flagship) is directly applicable to Ryan Realty. The Ryan Realty autonomous pipeline already approximates this: short news clips (elevated, weekly), listing reels (flagship, per listing), and social drops (everyday). Formalizing this as a three-tier production ladder in the Q3 strategy gives Matt a decision framework for where to invest production time.

## 7. Douglas Elliman

**Model:** Full-service publicly traded brokerage (NYSE: DOUG). Primarily New York, South Florida, California, and Texas. Approximately 6,500 agents. [Source: Douglas Elliman 2024 Annual Report.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- Douglas Elliman maintains a centralized marketing department that produces brand advertising, property brochures, and digital campaigns. Agents receive co-branded marketing support but within strict brand guidelines.
- Market reports are published quarterly under the "Elliman Report" brand, authored by appraiser Jonathan Miller. These reports are widely cited in media. They function as both content marketing and media relations. [Source: Elliman.com, Market Reports section, 2025.]
- Print advertising remains a budget line for Elliman in high-end lifestyle publications (WSJ Mansion, duPont Registry, New York Times Real Estate). [Source: Elliman 2024 Annual Report.]
- Digital advertising focuses on targeted display and social ads for high-net-worth audiences.

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** The "Elliman Report" model is the premium version of what Ryan Realty's monthly market report aims to build: a regularly published, citeable, named market intelligence product. At scale, market reports become media relations assets, not just content marketing. As Ryan Realty's reports build a track record of accuracy, they become pitchable to Bend Bulletin, KTVZ, and regional business publications. This is a Q4 or 2027 goal, not a Q3 tactic.

## 8. Christie's International Real Estate

**Model:** Luxury franchise network affiliated with Christie's auction house. Approximately 1,000 offices in 50 countries. US presence concentrated in resort and urban luxury markets. [Source: Christie's International Real Estate website, 2025.]

**Marketing playbook structure:**
- Christie's brand positioning is entirely built on the Christie's auction house provenance and the luxury-market credibility that flows from it. Affiliate brokerages (like Cascade Hasson Sotheby's competitor) license the Christie's name.
- Property marketing standards are enforced through a playbook provided to affiliate offices. Standards cover: minimum photography requirements, digital marketing spend per listing price tier, print formats, open house standards.
- The Christie's Luxury Defined Report is an annual global luxury residential market report widely distributed to high-net-worth networks. [Source: Christie's International Real Estate, Luxury Defined 2025.]
- Social strategy for affiliates emphasizes lifestyle content and aspirational imagery over data-first content. The Christie's playbook positions real estate as a luxury experience, not a data decision.

**Takeaway for Ryan Realty:** Christie's approach is deliberately the opposite of Ryan Realty's data-first positioning. This is useful competitive context. Cascade Hasson Sotheby's uses this same luxury-aspiration playbook in the Bend market. Ryan Realty's differentiation is data transparency and honest market analysis. When a seller is deciding between Ryan Realty and Cascade Sotheby's, the distinction should be clear: one shows them the real numbers, the other shows them aspirational photography. The Q3 strategy should make this contrast explicit in the positioning section without naming competitors directly.

## Cross-cutting synthesis

Five patterns appear across six or more of these eight models.

**Pattern 1: Market reports as authority builders.** The Agency, Elliman, Serhant, and Christie's all publish named market reports. These reports generate media coverage, build SERP authority for market queries, and create email opt-in reasons. Ryan Realty's monthly market report is already on this path. The Q3 strategy should formalize publishing cadence, SEO targeting, and media distribution for each report.

**Pattern 2: Content tier ladders.** Serhant's three-tier (everyday / elevated / flagship) and eXp's volume-first approach represent opposite ends of a spectrum. The Serhant model is more appropriate for a single-market principal-broker firm that competes on quality.

**Pattern 3: Agent-as-brand vs. brokerage-as-brand tension.** Compass, eXp, and Real rely on agent brands. The Agency, Serhant, and Christie's affiliates build brokerage brand with agent brands nested under it. Ryan Realty is in the brokerage-brand camp. The Q3 strategy should clarify how broker headshots and individual broker social presence relate to the @ryanrealtybend handle without creating a confused identity.

**Pattern 4: Pre-listing services as lead differentiators.** Compass Concierge is the high-capital version. Side partner brokerages often offer lighter versions. Ryan Realty can implement a no-cost-to-sell consultation and contractor coordination service as a branded pre-listing offer without Compass's capital structure.

**Pattern 5: Technology as an agent-retention tool, not a consumer brand.** Compass, eXp, Real, and Side all compete on technology for agent recruitment. Ryan Realty's autonomous pipeline is an internal competitive advantage that enables better, faster content and more systematic lead follow-up. It should be described to potential broker hires as a capability, not marketed directly to consumers.
