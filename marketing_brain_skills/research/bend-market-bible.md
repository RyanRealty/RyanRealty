## 1. Bend-area neighborhoods

Price data below reflects 2025 to early 2026 market conditions. Sources include Beacon Report (Beacon Appraisal Group, Donnie Montagner, state-certified residential appraiser), MLS of Central Oregon (COAR), Redfin market pages, and NeighborhoodScout. Producers must re-query Supabase `listings` table or `market_stats_cache` before embedding any figure in a deliverable.

### 1.1 NW Crossing (NorthWest Crossing)

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $900,000 to $1.3 million median, with a June 2025 Redfin-reported median of $1.1 million. [Source: Redfin Northwest Crossing housing market page, June 2025.]

**Dominant home types:** Modern craftsman single-family homes and townhomes, built from 2000 onward. Predominantly two-story, 2,000 to 3,000 sq ft. Energy-efficient construction is a baseline expectation.

**Lot size norms:** Smaller urban lots, typically 4,000 to 8,000 sq ft. The neighborhood trades lot size for walkability and proximity to Shevlin Park and the Deschutes River Trail system.

**Key amenities:** Commercial village with coffee shops, restaurants, and independent retail. Solar-powered street lighting and EV charging infrastructure. Access to Shevlin Park, the Old Mill District via trail, and Skyliner Road backcountry.

**School assignment:** Buckingham Elementary, Pacific Crest Middle School, Summit High School. [Source: Bend-La Pine Schools attendance area tool, bend.k12.or.us.]

**HOA reality:** HOA fees run $120 to $135 per month. [Source: Redfin listing data, 2025.] Rules govern exterior paint colors, landscaping standards, and vehicle storage. Non-compliance generates fines.

**What makes it distinct:** Purpose-planned in the 1990s as a walkable new-urbanist neighborhood adjacent to Shevlin Park. Among the few Bend neighborhoods where a resident can walk to coffee, a trail, and a park in under five minutes.

**Buyer profile:** Relocating tech workers, remote professionals, and families prioritizing walkability and school access. Out-of-state buyers from California, Washington, and Colorado are a consistent segment. MLS subdivision aliases include NorthWest Crossing, Skyliner Summit, Shevlin Ridge, Westside Pines, Westside Meadows, Valhalla Heights, Treeline Phase 1, and Outcrop. [Source: Ryan Realty Supabase `neighborhood_subdivisions` table, migration 20260515170000.]

**What this means for producers:** Listing-description producers include Buckingham Elementary by name and walk time to Shevlin Park. Market-report producers track this as a separate neighborhood segment from general NW Bend because its price trajectory diverges.

### 1.2 Old Bend

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $600,000 to $900,000 for classic Craftsman bungalows and mid-century homes. Condos near downtown trade between $400,000 and $700,000. Downtown Bend July 2025 Redfin median was $630,000. [Source: Redfin Downtown Bend housing market page, July 2025.]

**Dominant home types:** Early 20th-century Craftsman bungalows, Colonial Revival cottages, and a small inventory of newer condos. Many homes are on the historic register or have historic character without formal designation.

**Lot size norms:** Small to medium urban lots, typically 5,000 to 10,000 sq ft. River-fronting parcels on Mirror Pond are rare and command significant premium.

**Key amenities:** Drake Park, the Deschutes River Trail, downtown Bend restaurants and breweries, the Les Schwab Amphitheater, and the Old Mill District are all within walking distance.

**School assignment:** William E. Miller Elementary, Cascade Middle School, and Bend High School for most of the neighborhood.

**HOA reality:** Most parcels are not in HOAs. Condo complexes carry their own HOA structures. Historic overlay rules apply to some blocks.

**What makes it distinct:** The oldest residential fabric in Bend. Mirror Pond views. Walking distance to the downtown core. Limited inventory and low turnover because many owners hold for the long term.

**Buyer profile:** Buyers who prioritize character and location over square footage. Renovation buyers targeting older homes. Retirees downsizing within the market. Some second-home buyers seeking a pied-a-terre near downtown amenities.

**What this means for producers:** Listing-description producers lead with proximity to Drake Park and the Deschutes River Trail. They do not use the cliche descriptors on the banned-word list. They use the factual equivalent: construction year, lot proximity to the river in linear feet, walk time to the downtown core.

### 1.3 Tetherow

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $1 million to $3.5 million for standard homesites. Larger custom homes on the northern edge range from $3.5 million to $6.5 million. Reported median of $1.8 million for the community overall. [Source: Tetherow.com live section, Homes.com Tetherow neighborhood page, 2025.]

**Dominant home types:** Contemporary and modern mountain-style single-family homes. New construction continues in later phases. Lodges and smaller homes are also available in sub-communities within the resort.

**Lot size norms:** Varies by sub-community. Homesites range from 0.2 to over one acre depending on location within the master-planned development.

**Key amenities:** Tetherow Golf Course (David McLay Kidd design). Year-round community pool, fitness center, and lodge. Walking distance to Widgi Creek and Seven Peaks School.

**School assignment:** Outside the public school attendance boundary for most phases. Seven Peaks School (private) is adjacent. Public school assignment is generally Elk Meadow Elementary, Pacific Crest Middle, and Summit High.

**HOA reality:** Monthly HOA dues of $165 billed semi-annually for most homesites. [Source: Tetherow.com, 2025.] All residents are required to hold a Tetherow Club membership, either social or golf tier, in addition to HOA dues. Total carrying cost varies by membership level.

**What makes it distinct:** The only Bend residential community with an internationally recognized golf course as its backbone. Gated with 24-hour security. Purpose-built for full-time and part-time owners who want resort amenities without the full vacation-rental ecosystem that exists in Sunriver.

**Buyer profile:** High-net-worth buyers, often from California and Washington. Second-home purchasers. Retirees from high-income careers who want mountain-town access with resort infrastructure. MLS subdivision aliases in the Ryan Realty system include Tetherow, Sunrise Village, Westbrook Meadows, Braeburn, 1st On The Hillsites, Lodges at Bachelor V, Triple, Campbell Road, and Roald West. [Source: Supabase `neighborhood_subdivisions`, migration 20260515170000.]

**What this means for producers:** STR compliance rules apply inside the resort overlay. Listing producers must verify whether the specific homesite is in the STR-permitted zone before including STR income language in copy.

### 1.4 Broken Top

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $1.5 million to $5 million and above for custom estates on and near the golf course. [Source: Cascade Hasson Sotheby's Broken Top neighborhood page, 2026; Norada Real Estate market analysis, 2026.] A significant share of sales occurs off-market through broker networks.

**Dominant home types:** Custom estate homes, large single-family residences, and luxury townhomes. Construction spans late 1990s through recent remodel projects. The 2026 trend is interior modernization of original estates.

**Lot size norms:** One-half to two acres is common for golf course-fronting lots. Townhome lots are smaller.

**Key amenities:** Broken Top Club, 18-hole championship golf course (Tom Weiskopf and Jay Morrish design), clubhouse, pool, tennis courts, fitness center, and restaurant. 24-hour guarded gate.

**School assignment:** Juniper Elementary, Pacific Crest Middle, Summit High School.

**HOA reality:** Dual-fee structure. Residents pay HOA dues to the community association plus club membership fees. Social membership fees are mandatory for all homeowners. Golf membership is a separate election.

**What makes it distinct:** Guarded and gated, one of only two communities in Bend with a full-time security presence (alongside Vandevert Ranch). Golf course-fronting inventory is the most constrained sub-segment. Off-market transactions are common.

**Buyer profile:** Physicians, attorneys, tech executives, and retirees from high-earning careers. Second-home buyers who also own a primary residence outside Oregon. MLS aliases include Broken Top, Golden Butte, Parks At Broken Top, Overturf Butte, and The Highlands at Broken Top. [Source: Supabase `neighborhood_subdivisions`, migration 20260515170000.]

**What this means for producers:** Listing producers flag the dual-fee HOA structure explicitly. Market-report producers treat Broken Top as a separate data segment. Buyer-persona producers place this neighborhood in the $1.5M+ tier alongside Tetherow and Awbrey Butte.

### 1.5 Awbrey Butte

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $900,000 to $2.5 million. Median real estate price of approximately $1.2 million per NeighborhoodScout 2025 data. Luxury listings above $1.3 million median list price per Redfin 2025 data. Average home size 4,007 sq ft, average price per square foot approximately $485. [Source: NeighborhoodScout Awbrey Butte profile, 2025; Redfin Awbrey Butte housing market page, 2025.]

**Dominant home types:** Large single-family homes on half-acre to full-acre lots. Wide variety of architectural styles from the 1980s and 1990s through contemporary. Over 760 homesites across multiple sub-developments.

**Lot size norms:** One-half acre to one acre is the norm. Some lots exceed one acre with Cascade panoramic views.

**Key amenities:** Proximity to Awbrey Glen Golf Club. Shevlin Park access. 10-minute drive to downtown Bend. Cascade Mountain views are the primary amenity for many homesites.

**School assignment:** Elk Meadow Elementary for the western portions, Bear Creek Elementary for parts closer to NW Bend. Cascade Middle, and Bend Senior High for the northern portions.

**HOA reality:** Varies by sub-development. Some sub-developments have no HOA. Others, such as Awbrey Glen and Awbrey Court, have active HOAs with fees from $50 to $200 per month.

**What makes it distinct:** Established NW Bend hillside community with the largest inventory of view lots. Originally developed by Brooks Resources Corporation with attention to setbacks and view corridors. Properties held long-term by owners who value the view over urban walkability.

**Buyer profile:** Executives, established professionals, and retirees. Median age in the area is above the Bend-wide average. Buyers who want a large home and lot but are not within a gated resort community.

**What this means for producers:** Producers describe view exposure in compass terms and name visible Cascade peaks (Mount Bachelor, South Sister, Broken Top, Mount Jefferson) as specific facts rather than generic "mountain views."

### 1.6 Northpointe

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $475,000 to $600,000 for standard single-family homes. Specific 2024-2025 sales ranged from $475,000 (October 2024) to $529,500 (January 2025) for two- to three-bedroom homes at $415 to $419 per square foot. [Source: Homes.com Northpointe sold listings, 2025.]

**Dominant home types:** Ranch-style and two-story homes built from the late 1990s through 2010s. Lot sizes and home footprints are modest by Bend standards.

**Lot size norms:** 5,000 to 8,000 sq ft typical.

**Key amenities:** Close to Costco, big-box retail, Bend's north side commercial corridor, and convenient US 97 access.

**School assignment:** Juniper Elementary, Cascade Middle School, Bend Senior High School.

**HOA reality:** Many Northpointe sub-developments have no HOA. Others have minimal HOAs with dues under $50 per month.

**What makes it distinct:** Among the more affordable single-family neighborhoods with a Bend city address. Practical access to employment corridors and retail.

**Buyer profile:** First-time buyers. Healthcare workers employed at St. Charles who need a reasonable commute. Buyers priced out of the NW Bend and Westside markets.

**What this means for producers:** This neighborhood is a primary target for first-time-buyer content and seller-funnel messaging aimed at long-tenured owners who bought pre-2020 and hold substantial equity.

### 1.7 Northwest Bend (general)

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $700,000 to $1.1 million for standard single-family homes outside the named sub-communities. Homes in this area with Cascade views command premiums.

**Dominant home types:** Mix of mid-century ranches, 1980s and 1990s construction, and newer infill. Lot sizes are generally larger than the NW Crossing core.

**Key amenities:** Shevlin Park, Tumalo State Park access, Skyliner Road recreation, Awbrey Glen Golf Club.

**School assignment:** Elk Meadow Elementary, Pacific Crest Middle, Summit High.

**HOA reality:** Most parcels outside named subdivisions carry no HOA.

**What makes it distinct:** Generally quieter street patterns and larger lots than NW Crossing. Less walkable to commercial amenities. More popular with buyers who want a larger lot and privacy over urban walkability.

**Buyer profile:** Buyers moving up from smaller Bend homes. Families with outdoor recreation as a primary lifestyle driver. Buyers who compare NW Bend against NW Crossing and choose lot size.

**What this means for producers:** Content producers distinguish NW Bend from NW Crossing explicitly. They do not treat these as a single market segment. Price ranges differ by roughly $150,000 to $200,000 at the median.

### 1.8 Southeast Bend

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $500,000 to $750,000 for standard SFR. Below the city median for comparable square footage.

**Dominant home types:** Mid-century ranches, 1990s and 2000s subdivisions. Larger lot subdivisions like Larkspur (median $533,000 in September 2025, per Redfin) and Mountain View are the anchor segments. [Source: Redfin Larkspur housing market page, September 2025.]

**Lot size norms:** 6,000 to 14,000 sq ft typical. Some SE Bend parcels on rural transition edges exceed one acre.

**Key amenities:** Bend Senior High School, Pilot Butte State Scenic Viewpoint, Larkspur Park, and proximity to the east side commercial corridor (US 20).

**School assignment:** William E. Miller, Bear Creek, or Rosland Elementary depending on block. Marshall Middle School. Bend Senior High School or Mountain View High School.

**HOA reality:** Most subdivisions carry no HOA or only minimal HOAs.

**What makes it distinct:** The primary first-time-buyer and move-up market on the east side. Less westside cachet but more square footage per dollar. Improving commercial amenities as development has moved east.

**Buyer profile:** First-time buyers, healthcare workers, teachers, retail managers, and others in professional but not executive income brackets.

**What this means for producers:** Seller-funnel producers targeting long-held SE Bend homes should emphasize equity accumulation since 2019 purchase prices. A home bought for $350,000 in 2019 now likely trades above $550,000, representing over $200,000 in gross equity gain.

### 1.9 Three Rivers / Sunriver

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** Three Rivers area south of Sunriver, $400,000 to $650,000. Sunriver proper, $637,000 to $725,000 median (April and 12-month trailing respectively). [Source: Redfin Sunriver housing market page, April 2026; Sunriver Realty neighborhood page, 2025.] Crosswater community trades at $1.5 million and above.

**Dominant home types:** Single-family resort homes, vacation cabin configurations, condos and townhomes throughout the Sunriver Owners Association boundary. Caldera Springs features newer custom homes.

**Lot size norms:** Sunriver proper has compact resort lots. Three Rivers area parcels on the Deschutes River can be larger (one to five acres).

**Key amenities:** Sunriver Owners Association owns and operates SHARC (aquatic and recreation center), 40-plus miles of paved bike paths, two 18-hole golf courses, Sunriver Marina, Sunriver Stables, and Sunriver Airport. [Source: Sunriver Owners Association (SROA) official site, 2025.]

**School assignment:** Sunriver is in the Three Rivers School District (separate from Bend-La Pine Schools). Three Rivers School District serves La Pine, Sunriver, and surrounding areas. Students in the southern Three Rivers area and La Pine corridor may also fall within Three Rivers SD.

**HOA reality:** All Sunriver residential parcels pay monthly SROA dues, which vary by property type. Condo and townhome communities layer their own HOA dues on top of SROA dues. Three Rivers area outside the SROA boundary typically has no HOA.

**What makes it distinct:** Sunriver is a 3,300-acre resort development with 4,200 private homes, five miles of Deschutes River frontage, and an airport. [Source: Sunriver Realty neighborhood guide, 2025.] It functions as a self-contained resort town 15 miles south of Bend. STR activity is significant and legally permitted within certain sub-areas.

**Buyer profile:** STR investors. Retirees from California and Washington seeking a resort-lifestyle primary residence. Remote workers who want a car-optional resort environment with Bend proximity.

**What this means for producers:** STR-investor buyer persona applies here. Listing producers must include SROA dues in the cost-of-ownership section. Any STR income projection must cite actual permit status and current occupancy data, not estimates.

### 1.10 Tumalo

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $800,000 to $2 million for homes on larger agricultural parcels. Median reported at approximately $1.075 million by NeighborhoodScout, with high variance due to parcel size spread. [Source: NeighborhoodScout Tumalo profile, 2025; Redfin Tumalo housing market page, 2025.]

**Dominant home types:** Rural residential on large lots, agricultural parcels with ranch homes, and a growing inventory of custom homes on acreage. Manufactured homes on acreage also present.

**Lot size norms:** Five to 40 acres typical for agricultural properties. Smaller rural residential lots of one to five acres exist closer to the Tumalo townsite.

**Key amenities:** Tumalo State Park, Deschutes River access, Tumalo Creek, direct trail access to the Cascade Range backcountry. Tumalo Feed Company (well-known local restaurant). Proximity to Bend's commercial services while maintaining rural character.

**School assignment:** Tumalo Community School (public, K-8). High school students route to Bend Senior High or Summit High through Bend-La Pine Schools. [Source: Bend-La Pine Schools attendance area lookup.]

**HOA reality:** Rare outside specific rural subdivisions. Most Tumalo parcels are deed-restricted only by county zoning, not HOA CC&Rs.

**What makes it distinct:** Unincorporated Deschutes County community northwest of Bend. Agricultural zoning protects open space. Irrigation rights from Tumalo Irrigation District or Swalley Irrigation District are a significant property attribute in this area.

**Buyer profile:** Buyers seeking acreage, equestrian properties, or agricultural parcels. Remote workers who want rural character within 15 minutes of Bend services. Second-home buyers seeking a true pastoral setting. Ryan Realty has deep market knowledge here. The Tumalo Reservoir CMA (May 2026) is the canonical CMA exemplar.

**What this means for producers:** Listing producers must verify irrigation district membership for every Tumalo listing. Irrigated vs. non-irrigated status materially affects land value. The acreage and water attribution are lead data points, not footnotes.

### 1.11 Vandevert Ranch

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** Average near $3 million. [Source: Vandevert Ranch community profile, bendrealestate.com, 2025.] Low turnover and frequent off-market transactions make median tracking unreliable from public MLS data alone.

**Dominant home types:** Custom estate homes on a 400-acre gated ranch. Home count is small by design. Properties trade infrequently.

**Lot size norms:** Multi-acre equestrian parcels with shared ranch infrastructure.

**Key amenities:** Private stable, paddocks, riding area, pasture, and eight miles of private riding trails. Staff animal caretaker on site for horses and dogs. [Source: Vandevert Ranch Association (vandevertranch.org), 2025.] Deschutes River frontage within the ranch boundary.

**School assignment:** Three Rivers School District (south Deschutes County).

**HOA reality:** Governed by the Vandevert Ranch Association. Professional property management. Annual meeting of homeowner-elected board directors.

**What makes it distinct:** One of the rarest residential communities in Central Oregon. 400-acre footprint, gated, equestrian infrastructure, full-time ranch staff. No short-term rentals. No Airbnb. MLS alias is a single name: Vandevert Ranch. [Source: Supabase `neighborhood_subdivisions`, migration 20260515170000.]

**Buyer profile:** Ultra-high-net-worth buyers. Equestrian families. Buyers requiring extreme privacy. Off-market transactions through broker relationships are the primary path.

**What this means for producers:** Ryan Realty positions its off-market network competence at the $1M+ tier here. Any content referencing Vandevert Ranch avoids pricing language that cannot be verified from a live MLS pull.

### 1.12 Tillicum Village

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $450,000 to $650,000. A budget-accessible SE Bend neighborhood.

**Dominant home types:** Modest single-family homes, typically 1,400 to 2,200 sq ft, built in the late 1990s and 2000s.

**Lot size norms:** 6,000 to 9,000 sq ft typical.

**Key amenities:** Close to US 97, easy Bend-to-Redmond commute. Proximity to east side schools and retail.

**School assignment:** William E. Miller or Bear Creek Elementary, Marshall Middle, Mountain View High School.

**HOA reality:** Varies by block. Many parcels have no HOA.

**What makes it distinct:** One of the more affordable established neighborhoods with a Bend city address. Popular with buyers who need budget room to furnish and build equity.

**Buyer profile:** First-time buyers, households with one income, buyers relocating from higher-cost markets with limited equity to bring.

**What this means for producers:** Seller-funnel messaging here emphasizes equity unlocked since initial purchase. Buyers who entered between 2015 and 2020 at $250,000 to $380,000 now hold substantial equity. These are seller-lead targets.

### 1.13 Sundance

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $500,000 to $750,000.

**Dominant home types:** Suburban single-family homes, 1,600 to 2,800 sq ft, newer construction.

**Lot size norms:** 7,000 to 12,000 sq ft. Somewhat larger lots than the NE Bend core, reflecting SE Bend land availability at the time of development.

**Key amenities:** Proximity to Larkspur Park, Bend Senior High, and the east side commercial corridor.

**School assignment:** Rosland or Bear Creek Elementary, Marshall Middle, Bend Senior High School.

**HOA reality:** Minimal or no HOA in most blocks.

**What makes it distinct:** Part of the SE Bend suburban fabric. More elbow room than NW Crossing for roughly the same price. Less proximity to the Deschutes River trail system.

**Buyer profile:** Growing families seeking more square footage per dollar than the Westside offers. Move-up buyers from Tillicum Village or Northpointe.

**What this means for producers:** Producers pair Sundance and Larkspur as the SE Bend move-up corridor. Messaging emphasizes lot size and school access.

### 1.14 Mountain Village

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $500,000 to $750,000.

**Dominant home types:** 1990s to 2010s single-family homes. Mix of one-story ranch plans and two-story floor plans.

**Lot size norms:** 7,000 to 12,000 sq ft.

**Key amenities:** East side location near Mountain View High School.

**School assignment:** Rosland Elementary, Marshall Middle, Mountain View High School.

**HOA reality:** Minimal or no HOA in most sub-areas.

**What makes it distinct:** Primary feeder neighborhood for Mountain View High School. Proximity to the US 20 East Bend Bypass and the Deschutes River trail system on the eastern edge.

**Buyer profile:** Families prioritizing school district assignment to Mountain View High. Entry-level move-up buyers.

**What this means for producers:** Any listing in Mountain Village that is within the Mountain View attendance boundary should name Mountain View High School explicitly. School-assignment accuracy is a material fact.

### 1.15 Larkspur

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $533,000 median (September 2025, Redfin). Down 12.7% year-over-year. 12-month trailing median approximately $550,000. [Source: Redfin Larkspur housing market page, September 2025.]

**Dominant home types:** Mid-size suburban homes, mix of ranch and two-story plans, built primarily in the early 2000s.

**Lot size norms:** Near one acre on average per some sources, though many Larkspur lots are 8,000 to 15,000 sq ft. The neighborhood includes both standard subdivision lots and some larger semi-rural parcels on the outer edges.

**Key amenities:** Larkspur Park, access to Bend's east side trail network, proximity to Bend Senior High School.

**School assignment:** Rosland or Bear Creek Elementary, Marshall Middle, Bend Senior High School.

**HOA reality:** Many Larkspur blocks have no HOA. Some sub-plats have minimal HOA with dues under $50 per month.

**What makes it distinct:** SE Bend's largest established mid-market neighborhood. Median price point below the Bend overall median makes it a primary first-time and move-up corridor. Price softness since peak 2023 values creates negotiating room for buyers.

**Buyer profile:** First-time buyers, healthcare and education sector workers, and buyers relocating from higher-cost markets.

**What this means for producers:** Sellers who purchased before 2021 at prices below $400,000 are equity-rich despite recent price softness. They are a seller-lead target. Producers should frame the story around locking in equity gains rather than chasing a market peak.

### 1.16 La Pine

**Typical price range (2025-2026):** $355,000 median (November 2025, Redfin, up 3.1% year-over-year). [Source: Redfin La Pine housing market page, November 2025.] The most affordable incorporated community in Deschutes County.

**Dominant home types:** Mix of manufactured homes, modest SFR on standard lots, and newer construction. A high proportion of recently built homes due to the city's relatively recent incorporation and expansion.

**Lot size norms:** Standard residential lots of 7,500 to 12,000 sq ft in the incorporated area. Larger rural parcels on the edge of the city limits.

**Key amenities:** La Pine State Park, Newberry National Volcanic Monument, Little Deschutes River access, proximity to Cascade Lakes Highway recreation.

**School assignment:** La Pine schools within the Three Rivers School District.

**HOA reality:** Minimal presence in most areas.

**What makes it distinct:** Southernmost community in Deschutes County served by Ryan Realty. The strongest affordability story in the market area. Median age of housing stock is among the youngest in Oregon. La Pine incorporated as a city in 2006. Hispanic and Latino residents account for 14.38% of the population, the highest share among the communities Ryan Realty serves. [Source: NeighborhoodScout La Pine profile, 2025.]

**Buyer profile:** Price-sensitive buyers who work in Bend and commute. Retirees seeking affordable rural living. First-generation homebuyers.

**What this means for producers:** La Pine content must cite Three Rivers School District, not Bend-La Pine Schools. The school district boundary is a material fact for families. Language options in listing copy serve a higher-diversity audience here than in other Ryan Realty service areas.

## 4. Regulatory environment

### 4.1 Oregon property disclosure requirements

Oregon law (ORS 105.464 and ORS 105.465) requires sellers to provide a Seller's Property Disclosure Statement to any buyer making a written offer. [Source: ORS 105.464, oregon.public.law/statutes/ors_105.464.] The statement covers title and easements, systems (water, sewage, plumbing, electrical, HVAC), structure (roof, insulation, foundation, unpermitted additions), and community (HOA or shared areas).

After delivery, the buyer has five business days to revoke their offer, unless the buyer has waived that right in writing. [Source: ORS 105.464.]

**Lead-based paint disclosure (federal, applies to pre-1978 homes):** Sellers of homes built before 1978 must provide the EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family from Lead in Your Home" and give buyers a 10-day period for lead-based paint inspection unless waived in writing.

**Well disclosure:** For properties on a private well, sellers must disclose the well's age, water quality test history, and whether the well has run dry. Oregon Department of Human Services recommends testing before close. OWRD maintains the Water Resources Information System (WRIS) for well log records.

**Septic disclosure:** For properties on a septic system (common outside Bend city limits in Deschutes County), sellers disclose the system age, maintenance history, any failures, and drain field status. County records are the primary source.

**What this means for producers:** Listing-description producers do not include statements like "no known issues" unless confirmed by the disclosure form. Any listing on well and septic must note that status explicitly. Buyers should be directed to review the disclosure form.

### 4.2 City of Bend ADU policy

The City of Bend allows up to two accessory dwelling units (ADUs) on a residential lot with a single-unit dwelling, following Bend City Council approval in June 2025. [Source: City of Bend ADU Resource Hub, bendoregon.gov/services/development-permitting/adu-resources-hub; Bend Development Code 3.6.200.]

**Size limits:** First ADU maximum 800 sq ft. Second ADU maximum 500 sq ft. [Source: City of Bend ADU Code Requirements, December 2025 PDF.]

**Setbacks:** Detached ADU minimum 6 ft from the main structure, subject to exceptions. Standard setback rules apply to the property line.

**Parking:** No minimum on-site parking required for ADUs. [Source: City of Bend ADU Code Requirements, 2025.]

**Owner occupancy:** The 2025 code update removed prior owner-occupancy requirements for ADUs in many zones, expanding ADU development potential. Confirm current requirements per zone against the live Bend Development Code at bend.municipal.codes.

**Pre-approved designs:** The City released a set of pre-approved ADU design packages to streamline permitting. [Source: Central Oregon Daily, 2025.]

**Deschutes County rules (unincorporated areas, including Tumalo):** Differ from City of Bend rules. Producers working on rural Deschutes County listings must check county-specific ADU provisions separately from the Bend Development Code. [Source: Deschutes County Development Code.]

**What this means for producers:** Any listing with ADU potential is a content opportunity. Listing-description producers identify lot size and existing structures in relation to ADU potential and include a sentence noting the City's two-ADU allowance when applicable. This is a buyer-relevant fact that can expand the buyer pool.

### 4.3 Short-term rental rules

**City of Bend:** Governed by Bend Development Code Chapter 3.6.500, most recently amended by Ordinance NS-2541, codified December 2025. [Source: bend.municipal.codes/BDC/3.6.500.]

Key rules:
- Whole-house STRs rented for less than 30 days, where cumulative rental days total 30 or more per year, must maintain a 500-foot buffer from other whole-house STRs. [Source: Bend Development Code 3.6.500, December 2025.]
- Two permit types exist. Type I and Type II, with different fee structures and requirements by zoning district.
- Applications in RL, RS, RM, RH, and MR zoning districts (outside the Old Mill District) after November 4, 2021, are subject to the density restrictions.
- Application fees as of 2024: approximately $882 for Type I, $2,454 for Type II, plus a 4% surcharge. [Source: City of Bend STR program page, bendoregon.gov/services/business/short-term-rentals/.]

**Deschutes County (unincorporated areas):** Separate STR ordinance. Rural properties including many Tumalo, La Pine, and Three Rivers area parcels operate under county rather than city rules.

**Sunriver STR:** Sunriver Owners Association permits STR within specific SROA-approved parameters. High STR activity is normal and legal in many Sunriver sub-areas, including individual homeowner rentals through platforms like VRBO and Airbnb.

**What this means for producers:** STR income language in listing copy requires verification of permit status. Producers cannot assert STR income potential without confirming that a valid permit exists or that the property is in a permitted zone. This is a compliance risk. Per CLAUDE.md §0, unverified claims do not ship.

### 4.4 Water rights

**Surface water:** By 1905, rights to more water had been issued in the Deschutes Basin than the river carries during summer months. [Source: Deschutes River Conservancy FAQ, deschutesriver.org.] Water rights operate under prior appropriation (first in time, first in right). Senior irrigators, including the Central Oregon Irrigation District (COID), the Tumalo Irrigation District, and the Swalley Irrigation District, hold the oldest rights and have priority in dry years.

**Groundwater:** The Deschutes Groundwater Mitigation Program (OWRD) requires any new groundwater permit within the Deschutes Groundwater Study Area to provide mitigation for impacts on surface water flows. The US Geological Survey confirmed a hydraulic connection between groundwater and surface water in the basin. [Source: OWRD Deschutes Groundwater Mitigation Program, oregon.gov/owrd.]

A mitigation credit system exists where applicants must secure mitigation water equal to their intended use before a permit issues. [Source: OWRD Mitigation Credits page, 2025.]

**Well permitting:** New domestic wells in Deschutes County require an OWRD permit, a well driller licensed in Oregon, and compliance with the Groundwater Mitigation Program if the well is within the study area.

**Irrigation district membership:** Properties within COID, Tumalo Irrigation District, or Swalley Irrigation District boundaries typically receive piped or ditch-delivered surface water rights appurtenant to the land. Membership carries annual assessments and the right to receive allocated water. Sellers must disclose irrigation district membership and water delivery history.

**What this means for producers:** Any rural or agricultural listing must include irrigation district membership status as a material fact. Listings marketed for farming, livestock, or large landscaping must note water source and legal right to that water. This is a disclosure compliance matter, not just a marketing point.

### 4.5 Wildfire defensible space requirements

Oregon Senate Bill 762 (enacted 2021) established the framework for statewide wildfire risk mapping and defensible space requirements. [Source: Oregon Department of Forestry wildfire hazard page, oregon.gov/odf/fire.]

Properties located in both the Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) and in a High Hazard designation are subject to OSFM defensible space codes or fire-hardening building codes. [Source: Oregon State Fire Marshal Homeowner Guide 2025, oregon.gov/osfm.]

Property owners with tax lots in both High Hazard and WUI categories received certified mail notice on January 7, 2025. [Source: Oregon Property Owners Association reporting, January 2025.]

Central Deschutes County, including parts of Tumalo, La Pine, and rural areas surrounding Bend, contains a high proportion of WUI and High Hazard properties.

**What defensible space requires:**
- Zone 0 (0 to 5 ft from structure): Non-combustible materials only.
- Zone 1 (5 to 30 ft): Maintained, well-irrigated, low-fuel landscaping.
- Zone 2 (30 to 100 ft): Reduced fuel load, tree limbing, spacing between plants.

**What this means for producers:** Rural listings in the WUI must disclose wildfire risk. Content about rural properties should not downplay fire risk, and any buyer guidance content about rural Deschutes County must reference SB 762 compliance and OSFM resources. Fire insurance availability and cost is a material factor for buyers of WUI-adjacent properties.

### 4.6 HOA and CC&R highlights for major communities

**NW Crossing:** CC&Rs require exterior color scheme approval from the architectural review committee. Modifications to landscaping visible from the street require approval. HOA dues fund common area maintenance and community event infrastructure.

**Tetherow Homeowners Association:** Club membership requirement for all owners is embedded in CC&Rs. Any buyer waiving the club membership requirement at closing would be in violation. This must be disclosed in listing copy.

**Broken Top HOA:** Dual-fee structure (HOA plus club membership) is governed by recorded CC&Rs. Gate and security are funded by HOA dues. Rental restrictions limit non-owner occupancy periods. Confirm current rental restriction status before including STR language.

**Vandevert Ranch CC&Rs:** Ranch operational rules govern livestock, trail use, and equestrian facilities. Very limited commercial activity permitted. Confirm current restrictions with the Vandevert Ranch Association directly before any listing goes live.

## 5. COCAR and MLS rules

### 5.1 Organization

The Central Oregon Association of REALTORS (COCAR) operates the MLS of Central Oregon, the primary MLS for Deschutes, Crook, and Jefferson County real estate transactions. [Source: COCAR, coar.com.] All agents licensed by Ryan Realty operate under COAR membership and the MLS of Central Oregon.

### 5.2 Clear Cooperation Policy

The National Association of REALTORS (NAR) Clear Cooperation Policy requires MLS members to submit a listing to the MLS within one business day of publicly marketing a property. [Source: NAR Clear Cooperation Policy, nar.realtor.] Public marketing includes yard signs, social media, and email blasts.

The 2024 NAR settlement and subsequent rule changes (effective August 2024 in most markets) introduced seller-signed buyer-broker agreement requirements before showing property. [Source: NAR settlement reporting, Bend Bulletin, 2024.] COAR enforces these requirements for MLS-member brokers.

An office exclusive exemption permits a property to be listed as an MLS "office exclusive" and not entered into the full MLS for a defined period, but this restricts marketing to only the listing broker's clients. Ryan Realty uses this option selectively for seller clients who specifically request a pre-market period.

**Important note on "off-market" language:** Content that describes a property as "off-market" in a public-facing context (social posts, website, emails to non-clients) when it has not been formally entered into the MLS as an office exclusive, or when it has been publicly marketed, may violate Clear Cooperation. Producers must not use "off-market" framing in public content. [Source: NAR Clear Cooperation Policy interpretation; CLAUDE.md brand voice rules.]

### 5.3 Market statistics reporting

COAR publishes quarterly market statistics derived from the MLS of Central Oregon. The Beacon Report (Section 9) provides the most-used independent monthly view. Producers who need verified market data should pull from Supabase `market_stats_cache` or `market_pulse_live`, which are populated from the Spark API feed from the MLS. COAR's own statistics page at coar.com/consumer-info/market-statistics provides additional public data.

**What this means for producers:** Producers cite COAR or the Beacon Report as the named source for any MLS-derived statistic. They do not cite generic "market data" without a named source. Per CLAUDE.md §0, a one-line verification trace is required for each figure.

## 6. Migration patterns

### 6.1 Population growth

Deschutes County population: 213,886 as of July 1, 2025 (Census Bureau estimate). The county added 2,764 residents in the year ended July 1, 2025. Since 2010, population grew 37.5%. Since 2019, growth was 10.8%. [Source: Census Bureau QuickFacts, Deschutes County, 2025; KTVZ reporting March 2026.]

World Population Review estimates the 2026 county population at approximately 216,891. [Source: worldpopulationreview.com/us-counties/oregon/deschutes-county, 2026.]

### 6.2 Migration sources

- Total in-migrants: 13,327
- Total out-migrants: 11,479
- Net: +1,848

Top five origin counties for in-migration:
1. Multnomah County, OR: 1,004
2. Washington County, OR: 772
3. Clackamas County, OR: 592
4. Lane County, OR: 481
5. King County, WA: 446

Of total in-migrants, 5,598 came from within Oregon counties and 7,729 came from out of state. [Source: Oregon Employment Department, QualityInfo.org Deschutes County Migration IRS analysis, 2024, covering filing years 1991 through 2022.]

### 6.3 Household income of in-migrants

Deschutes County median household income: approximately $87,640 to $97,163 depending on ACS vintage. [Source: Census Bureau ACS 1-year 2023; FRED series MHIOR41017A052NCEN.] Homeownership rate: 70.7%, above the national 65% average. Poverty rate: approximately 8.6% to 8.8%. [Source: Census Bureau ACS, 2023.]

The incoming migration skews toward higher income brackets. In-migrants from the San Francisco Bay Area, Los Angeles, and Seattle tech markets bring significantly above-median household incomes by Bend standards, which has contributed to the Bend median home price reaching levels above the Oregon median by a substantial margin.

### 6.4 Implications for buyer personas

Out-of-state buyers arriving from California, Washington, and Colorado represent the segment most likely to be all-cash or high-equity buyers in the $750,000 to $2 million range. They typically have sold a primary residence in a higher-cost market and are seeking a permanent relocation or second home. Average tenure before resale is longer than for local move-up buyers.

**What this means for producers:** Seller-funnel content targeting out-of-state owners of Central Oregon property (second-home holders, investment property owners) should note that this segment is well-documented. Content addressing property management, tax exposure, and 1031 exchange opportunities for out-of-state sellers performs consistently.

## 7. Tourism and STR market dynamics

### 7.1 Visit Bend tourism statistics

Travel spending in the City of Bend: $382.2 million in 2022, $383.4 million in 2023 (increase of 0.2%). Lodging sales: $140 million in 2023, a 3.1% decrease from 2022. [Source: Visit Bend economic impact data, cited in Bend Bulletin, November 2025.]

The transient room tax (TRT) rate is 10.4% in Bend. Of TRT revenue, approximately 64.6% goes to the City General Fund. Both the City of Bend and Deschutes County retain roughly 70% of lodging tax revenue, with Visit Bend and Visit Central Oregon receiving approximately 30%. [Source: City of Bend Tourism page, bendoregon.gov/departments/community-development/economic-development-division/tourism/; KTVZ, March 2025.]

Active STR listings in Bend: approximately 1,779 as of 2025. [Source: AirDNA MarketMinder, airdna.co/vacation-rental-data/app/us/oregon/bend, 2025.]

Average annual STR revenue: $37,587 to $48,000 depending on property type and source. [Source: Airbtics and AirROI reporting, September 2024 through August 2025.]

Average daily rate (ADR): $201 to $270. Peak month occupancy in July. Low-season month: November. Peak monthly revenue: $7,278. [Source: AirROI Bend STR market report, 2025-2026.]

### 7.3 STR impact on long-term rental supply

Bend's 1,779-plus active STR units represent inventory removed from the long-term rental market. The 500-foot separation rule for new whole-house STR permits (December 2025 code update) has effectively capped new permit issuance in many residential zones. Existing permit holders are protected.

Long-term rental vacancy in Bend is tight. Multifamily development has not kept pace with population growth.

**What this means for producers:** STR-investor buyer persona is viable for Sunriver, select Bend properties with existing permits, and rural Deschutes County properties outside the city STR cap. Producers creating investor-buyer content must include the current permit status and the 500-foot rule as context before presenting any STR income projection.

- Median sale price by community (SFR only, typically)
- Year-over-year price comparison
- Active listing inventory
- Sales volume (closed transactions)
- Days on market (median)
- Long-term price trend analysis (Donnie Montagner maintains data back to 1997 for Bend SFR)

Special studies appear periodically, such as the Bend SFR study by square footage range (1,800 to 2,200 sq ft, July 2023) and Notice of Default tracking for Deschutes County.

### 9.3 Where to find it

PDF downloads at beaconappraisers.com/market-overviews/. Reports available for January 2015 through the current month. The October 2025 report is publicly available at beaconappraisers.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/BEACON-REPORT-October-2025.pdf. [Source: Beacon Appraisal Group website.]
